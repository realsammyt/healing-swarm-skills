Who are you right now?

A companion for your family

Your family is meeting something that feels impossible to name. A parent whose mind is changing. Children who sense it before anyone explains. A love that has to stretch across a gap that was never supposed to open.

You are not the first family to carry this. Across centuries and cultures — Jewish, Buddhist, Christian, Islamic, Indigenous, Japanese, African, secular — humans have met progressive cognitive change, forced separation, and anticipatory grief. They developed practices for it. Small, holdable, repeatable things. Ways to be present when memory fades. Ways to keep a family whole in a new shape.

This app gathers what they found.

What this is

36 practices organized into three modes — one for the family together, one for the father's own rhythm, one for the mother's private container. Each practice is simple enough that a tired person can follow it at the end of a long day. Each is grounded in research and in traditional wisdom. Each uses the words "may help" — because no practice is a prescription, and nothing here poses as a cure.

What this is not

  • Not a treatment plan. Not a diagnosis. Not a substitute for your family therapist, your doctor, or your court-appointed supervisor.
  • Not a cure. "Healing" here means preserving connection, dignity, presence, and the capacity to metabolize grief — not restoring what has changed.
  • Not a surveillance tool. Nothing you do in this app is tracked, sent, or stored anywhere except on this device. There are no accounts, no analytics, no data collection. Your data is yours.

How it was built

A research synthesis drew on anticipatory grief literature, attachment theory, dementia-care science, polyvagal theory, and the ritual and healing practices of more than a dozen traditions. Six parallel research threads produced the evidence base. Every citation was verified. Three independent reviewers — ethics, clinical, cultural — reviewed the material before any practice was written.

The practices were then translated into plain-language protocols, each with safety checks, signals to slow down or stop, modifications for hard days, and explicit lineage attribution for every tradition referenced. The app serves those protocols through three modes tuned to different users — the family, the father, the mother.

How to use it

  1. Pick your mode. "Who are you right now?" is the only question. Tap the one that fits today. You can always switch.
  2. Start with one practice. Not six. Not a program. One practice, for one week, done as consistently as you can. That is the entry level.
  3. Stopping is a win. Any practice can be paused at any step. Any week can be skipped. The app will be here when you come back.
  4. Install it. Add this page to your home screen (on iPhone: Share → Add to Home Screen; on Android: menu → Install App). After that, it works completely offline — no internet needed.

Privacy

This app was built with a principle called "privacy as sanctuary." Given that your family may involve court proceedings, supervised visitation, or sensitive clinical situations, the app is designed so that your data never leaves this device. There are no servers, no logins, no cookies, no tracking pixels, no analytics. The "What I felt today" log, your visit schedule, your grief-window times — all stored locally on your phone and nowhere else. You can export your data as a file if you want a backup. You can delete everything with one tap. No one else can see it.

If you need help right now

If you or someone in your family is in crisis — thoughts of self-harm, inability to care for the children, acute distress that will not settle — call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, US, 24/7). The Mother mode also has an "I'm at the edge" button that connects you to 988 and your trusted contacts in one tap.

You are not alone. You are not without precedent. The walk is the point — and the practices here may help you walk it.

Take what helps. Set down what doesn't. Your instinct about what will work for your family is more accurate than any app's.
