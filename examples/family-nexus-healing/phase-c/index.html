Who needs this right now?

A companion for your family

Your family is carrying something that doesn't have an easy name. A parent whose mind is changing. Children who feel it before anyone explains. A love that has to find a new shape.

You are not the first family to walk this. Across centuries and cultures — Jewish, Buddhist, Christian, Islamic, Indigenous, Japanese, African, secular — families have met this same territory. They found practices for it. Small things. Holdable things. Ways to be present when memory fades. Ways to stay whole in a new shape.

This app gathers what they found.

What this is

36 practices in three modes — one for the family together, one for the father, one for the mother. Each one is simple enough for a tired person at the end of a long day. Each is grounded in research and traditional wisdom. Each says "may help" — because nothing here is a prescription, and nothing poses as a cure.

What this is not

  • Not a treatment plan, diagnosis, or substitute for your therapist, doctor, or supervisor.
  • Not a cure. "Healing" here means preserving connection, dignity, and the capacity to move through grief — not restoring what has changed.
  • Not a surveillance tool. Nothing leaves this device. No accounts, no analytics, no tracking. Your data is yours alone.

How it was built

The practices draw on grief research, attachment theory, dementia-care science, polyvagal theory, and the healing traditions of more than a dozen cultures. Every citation was verified. Ethics, clinical, and cultural reviewers checked the material before any practice was written.

Each practice includes safety checks, signals to slow down or stop, modifications for hard days, and attribution for every tradition referenced.

How to use it

  1. Pick your mode. Tap the one that fits today. You can always switch.
  2. One practice. Not six. Not a program. One practice, done when you can. That is enough.
  3. Stopping is a win. Pause at any step. Skip any week. This will be here when you come back.
  4. Install it. Add to your home screen (iPhone: Share > Add to Home Screen; Android: menu > Install). Then it works offline — no internet needed.

Privacy

Your data never leaves this device. No servers, no logins, no cookies, no tracking. Everything — your felt-log, visit schedule, grief-window times — stays on your phone and nowhere else. You can export a backup or delete everything with one tap. No one else can see it.

If you need help right now

Call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, US, 24/7). The Mother mode has a button that connects you to 988 and your trusted contacts in one tap.
