**Date:** 2026-04-11

## Ethic 1: First, do no harm

**Verdict:** PASS WITH REVISIONS

The synthesis demonstrates unusually strong safety awareness. Explicit protective structures include: §1.2 disclaiming treatment-plan status, cure, and substitution for the care team; §1.6 wiring every future Phase B protocol to the RECOGNIZE · STOP · GROUND · ASSESS · RESPOND · FOLLOW-UP crisis framework and to an escalation path (988, local crisis lines, one trusted contact); §3.3 establishing "signals to slow or stop" as load-bearing for each protocol; §3.3's critical protective rule "if the father cannot, today, do this thing with ease and consent, it is not done today" and "the father should be protected from *performing* his own decline in front of his children"; §3.4's warning signs for the mother with the reframe "the edge is not the end; it is information"; §5's Configuration Matrices opening each configuration with explicit Absolute/Relative/Caution contraindication flags; §3.5's "readable by a supervisor" principle linked to child-welfare research on family secrets; §5.3 correctly ruling out formal "therapy session" framing for the 5-year-old; §5.4 explicitly listing parentification-inducing requests as Absolute contraindications for the 8-year-old.

**Three safety concerns require revision:**

1. **Self-harm/suicidality signal gap for the father.** §3.3's signals list is framed around agitation/withdrawal within a visit. It does not address the elevated suicide risk documented in younger-onset dementia populations, particularly post-diagnosis and at capability-loss transitions. A father under 50 losing identity, role, and independence while separated from his children by court order is a population for whom suicide risk should be named explicitly, with an escalation path paralleling the mother's.

2. **"Old photos" Caution flag is underspecified.** §5.6 flags looking at old photos as Caution but does not specify *who decides* whether to proceed, *what signs look like in real time during a supervised visit*, or *how the supervisor should be briefed*. For a configuration built first in Phase B, this flag needs operational detail.

3. **Small photo book in §3.5 and §5.6** is listed as a candidate practice without inheriting the §5.6 Caution on photos. A prepared photo book is a photo-viewing activity and should carry the same Caution.

## Ethic 2: Honor all traditions

**Verdict:** PASS WITH REVISIONS

This is the synthesis's strongest ethical dimension. The author consistently applies attribution discipline: §2.3 explicitly names that closed practices are not taught; §3.3 places an explicit caveat on Dzogchen rigpa; §3.5 treats Indigenous council traditions as examples "noted as examples of structured witnessed encounter, not as techniques to be extracted"; §3.6 contains a full "Appropriation warning" subsection; Linda Tuhiwai Smith is cited as the authority for Indigenous caveats; divergences are honored rather than smoothed.

**However, several items require revision:**

1. **"Celtic caoineadh" in §3.1 and §4.2** is cited without a specific contemporary Irish-language source. Phase B must not prescribe "keening" as a caregiver practice without an identifiable source with community consent or an explicit reframe as historical example only.
2. **"Grandmother-keeper traditions (various Indigenous lineages)" in §3.4** is vague. Either name a specific published source or remove the reference. Vagueness is itself a form of appropriation.
3. **"Latin American practices" and "Celtic traditions" for including children in loss (§3.2)** need specific authors or historical-reference framing.
4. **"Mexican luto"** needs a Mexican-authored or community-sourced reference distinct from Day of the Dead.
5. **The Lakota phrase *Mitákuye Oyás'iŋ*** in §3.6 is presented without source attribution.
6. **"Punshon 1984 Quaker visiting"** — resolve or reframe to rely on Pink Dandelion 2008.

## Ethic 3: Evidence with humility

**Verdict:** PASS

The synthesis consistently meets the "may help, not will cure" bar. §1.2 states "'Healing' in this document means preserving connection, dignity, presence, and the capacity to metabolize grief — not restoring lost cognitive function" — one of the most important single sentences in the document, correctly placed. Chapter-level claims are proportional. The Coelho & Barbosa finding is cited with its conditional ("associated with better post-loss adjustment *when accompanied by adequate support*"). §3.3 introduces its central claim with "convergent answer" and "may have never run primarily through narrative in the first place" — carefully hedged. §6.5 contains a formal [verification needed] consolidated list flagging 24 sources, with the honest note "None is believed to be fabricated, but all should be confirmed."

**No revisions required.**

## Ethic 4: Empower, don't control

**Verdict:** PASS

The synthesis's framing consistently treats the family as autonomous: §1 "The walk belongs to the family"; §1 to-the-mother note "nothing in this document is a verdict. Take what helps. Set down what doesn't"; §2.4 explicit permission to set things down; §3.3 adopts Swaffer's critique of prescribed disengagement and centers the father as agent ("he is a person with a diagnosis, not a diagnosis with a person attached... He is not managed; he is *supported*"); §4.7 elevates "dignity-through-role rather than dignity-through-sheltering" to load-bearing; §5 uses "candidate practices" throughout.

The synthesis resists the two most common failure modes: it does not assume the mother is "failing to cope," and it does not assume the father is "beyond reach."

**No revisions required.**

## Ethic 5: Privacy as sanctuary

**Verdict:** PASS WITH REVISIONS

Privacy-aware at the structural level: §1.3 item 5 names privacy as load-bearing; §1.5 names Phase C as load-bearing given discoverability; §3.5 addresses the privacy-and-secrecy tension correctly.

**However, several concerns arise at the self-discoverability level:**

1. **The synthesis reveals extensive identifying detail** about a specific family in a specific court proceeding. If this document is intended for inclusion in a public repository, demographic specificity could be correlated with a real family's filings. Clarify whether the family is hypothetical or specific-with-altered-details.
2. **If the family is real,** this document may not be committable to a public GitHub repository in its current form. The ethical bar is "could the combination of details re-identify."
3. **The synthesis is silent on whether the mother has seen this document and consented** to its contents about her anger and edge states.
4. **§3.4's "warning signs" inventory for the mother** is clinically appropriate but could be weaponized if discoverable in a contested proceeding. Phase B handoff documents should not repeat this list as an observation log.
5. **The supervisor-facing material in §3.5** does not specify what the family does if the supervisor does not adopt the "welcome witness" framing.

## Ethic 6: Accessible to all

**Verdict:** PASS WITH REVISIONS

The synthesis is written with unusual tonal care. The §1 "how to read" note distinguishes clinician, mother, and supervisor audiences. §1 framing and §2 meet the mother where she likely is — no jargon, no clinical register, no performance of expertise. It is a document a grieving mother could read without being patronized.

**However, §3.1–3.6 drift from this register.** They use technical vocabulary (anticipatory mourning, Strange Situation, polyvagal theory, shikantaza, rigpa, dhikr khafi, nirvikalpa samadhi, apophatic, communitas, liminal, etc.) without on-the-spot definitions. The gift of the chapters is buried under jargon. The stated goal "clinician-readable, mother-accessible" is currently over-promised.

**Recommendation:** Either gloss every technical term on first use in §3.1–3.6, OR revise §1 to clarify honestly that the body chapters are clinician-readable while the Phase B one-page handoff and the Phase C Mother mode are where the mother meets the material in accessible form. The reviewer prefers the honest audience clarification because glossing would double the document's length.

## Ethic 7: Continuous improvement

**Verdict:** PASS WITH REVISIONS

The synthesis leaves room for iteration in several correct ways: §1.3 item 7 commits to the principle; §5's progression stubs include Entry/Middle/Deepening phases; §6.5's consolidated [verification needed] list builds revision into the workflow; §3.3 names a specific axis of revision ("as the father's condition progresses, practices shift *toward simpler*"); §1.2 names Phase C's architectural accommodation.

**However:**

1. **§1.3 item 7 references "the progression logic in §6.2"** but §6.2 is the bibliography's "Advocacy and lived-experience sources." **Broken internal reference.** Fix to "§5's progression stubs."
2. **No explicit feedback mechanism.** The synthesis commits to revision in principle but does not specify how the family tells the project what isn't working.
3. **No sunset/retirement criteria for practices.** Progression stubs say practices should "shift toward simpler" but do not say when a practice should be retired entirely.

## Summary

This is a substantive, careful, ethically serious document. The author has clearly internalized the seven healing-swarm ethics and worked them into the structure of the synthesis. The overall tone is correct: humble, specific, honest about limits, centered on the family's autonomy, refusing prescription.

Strongest ethical dimensions: Evidence with humility (§3 and §6 handle verification cleanly) and Empower, don't control ("the walk belongs to the family" is consistent throughout).

Weakest ethical dimensions: Accessible to all (body chapters drift into jargon) and Privacy as sanctuary (the synthesis has not reckoned with its own discoverability if committed to a public repository).

The Honor all traditions dimension is mostly strong but has specific attribution gaps that must be sourced or removed.

The First, do no harm dimension is mostly strong but has a notable gap around suicide risk for the father and underspecified Caution flags around photo-based activities.

**None of the revision requests requires structural rework. All are surgical.** The synthesis advances to Clinical Review.
