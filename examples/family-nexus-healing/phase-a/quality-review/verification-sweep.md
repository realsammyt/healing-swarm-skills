### 6. DAI supervised-visitation specificity — REMOVE
Dementia Alliance International is correctly characterized elsewhere as an advocacy organization run by people with a dementia diagnosis, but **DAI does NOT produce resources specific to supervised-visitation, court-involvement, or child-custody contexts**. Any implication otherwise should be removed from the synthesis. DAI's value for this family is general dementia-advocacy and disability-rights framing; the supervised-visitation context must be adapted by the family themselves.

### 7. Finkelhor 1984 — REPHRASE (scope mismatch)

(1984). *Child Sexual Abuse: New Theory and Research.* Free Press. **Book confirmed.** However, the specific claim about "children asked to hold secrets from a parent are placed in loyalty binds with measurable harm" is more closely associated with the parentification literature (Chase 1999; Earley & Cushway 2002, both already in the bibliography) than with Finkelhor 1984, which is about CSA specifically. **Re-attribute the loyalty-bind claim to Chase 1999 / Earley & Cushway 2002**; Finkelhor can be cited only narrowly for the CSA-disclosure dynamic if at all.

### 14. Nhat Hanh child-oriented title — REPHRASE
*No Death, No Fear* (2002, Riverhead) is Nhat Hanh's adult treatment. For specifically child-oriented impermanence teaching, **better sources:**
- Nhat Hanh, T. (2001). *A Pebble for Your Pocket: Mindful Stories for Children and Grown-ups.* Plum Blossom Books / Parallax Press.
- Nhat Hanh, T. (2014). *Is Nothing Something? Kids' Questions and Zen Answers About Life, Death, Family, Friendship, and Everything in Between.* Plum Blossom Books.

Keep *No Death, No Fear* for adult context; add the child-oriented titles when the audience is children.

### 15. Caoineadh / Irish folklore sources — REPHRASE
Ó Crualaoich is a real Irish folklorist, but his major book *The Book of the Cailleach: Stories of the Wise-Woman Healer* (Cork University Press, 2003) is about the Cailleach/wise-woman figure, not primarily about caoineadh. **Better source for caoineadh specifically:**
- **Lysaght, P. (1997). Caoineadh os cionn coirp: The lament for the dead in Ireland. *Folklore, 108*(1–2), 65–82. doi:10.1080/0015587X.1997.9715938.**

Replace Ó Crualaoich with Lysaght 1997 for the specific caoineadh claim.

### 16. Pulido, Forrester & Lacina 2011 — CONFIRMED (with title correction)
Pulido, M. L., Forrester, S. P., & Lacina, J. M. (2011). Raising the bar: Why supervised visitation providers should be required to meet standards for service provision. *Family Court Review, 49*(2), 379–387. doi:10.1111/j.1744-1617.2011.01377.x.

**Note on scope:** The paper argues for provider standards and training as prerequisites for quality supervised visitation. It is NOT a direct study of "structural features correlated with successful supervised visits" as thread-5 implies. Soften the attribution: "Pulido, Forrester & Lacina (2011) argue for provider standards and training as prerequisites for quality supervised visitation."
