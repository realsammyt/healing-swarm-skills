# How to Use This Kit

*An entry-point document for the Family Nexus Healing protocols kit. Read this first.*

---

## What this kit is

This is a set of ready-to-run protocols — concrete practices organized by family configuration — designed to support a specific family facing younger-onset progressive cognitive change, court-ordered supervised visitation, and anticipatory grief. The protocols are drawn from six research threads that synthesized evidence-based research and traditional healing wisdom across many cultures. The full research is available at `../phase-a/phase-a-synthesis.md` and in the thread files under `../phase-a/research/`.

This kit is **supportive material to complement** the family's professional care. It is not a treatment plan. It is not a cure. It is not a substitute for the family's clinicians, family therapist, or court-appointed visit supervisor.

## Read these first, in this order

1. **This document** (`00-how-to-use-this-kit.md`) — you're reading it. ~10 minutes.
2. **`00-crisis-response-card.md`** — the crisis framework takes precedence over every other practice in this kit. If anyone in the family is in acute distress, this card applies immediately. Read it once so you know it exists, even if you do not need it today.
3. **`00-triage-one-page.md`** — asks "what is the hottest pain right now?" and maps to ONE starting configuration and ONE starting practice. Not all six configurations at once. One practice, one week, then check back.
4. **`00-contraindications-quick-ref.md`** — when NOT to use specific practices. Read before starting any practice.
5. **`00-clinician-handoff-sheet.md`** — a one-page document to bring to the family therapist, visit supervisor, or family physician. This is the only document in the kit designed to leave the household.
6. **`00-when-things-change.md`** — how to re-triage when the father's condition progresses, when a child's baseline shifts, or when the mother's capacity changes.

## The six configurations

The kit is organized into six directories, each holding practices for a specific relational configuration:

- **`father-with-kids-supervised/`** — practices for supervised visits between the father and the children. This was built first because it is the hardest and highest-stakes configuration.
- **`mother-with-kids/`** — daily rhythms (morning, after-school, bedtime), scripts for hard conversations, emergency co-regulation.
- **`each-child-alone/`** — developmentally differentiated practices for the 5-year-old boy and the 8-year-old girl separately.
- **`father-alone/`** — the father's own daily dignity rhythm, pre-visit centering, and connection-capsule practices (letters, voice recordings) for the children to carry forward.
- **`mother-alone/`** — the mother's own container work: daily anchor, weekly grief window, weekly rest container, fast resets, peer connection, escalation path.
- **`family-together/`** — rare, aspirational small rituals for seasonal or milestone moments.

## The archetype role-pack frame

Throughout the kit, you will see language that honors a specific energetic distinction: **holding-parent** (the one carrying the daily container — in this family, the mother), **changing-parent** (the one whose cognition is shifting — in this family, the father), and **children**. Some protocols include a `Holding-parent voice` and a `Changing-parent voice` subsection where the energy of the practice is role-specific.

This pattern exists so that the flagship build can preserve specific mother-energy and father-energy without flattening into generic "caregiver" language, AND so that future families with different configurations (reversed, same-gender, extended-family-as-holder) could be served by a variant role pack without rewriting the protocols. For this family, the assignments are explicit: holding-parent = mother, changing-parent = father.

## The protocol template — what every protocol looks like

Every protocol in the kit follows the same structure so you always know what you are getting:

- **Title, Configuration, Duration, Energy cost**
- **Why this practice** — one paragraph on the reasoning, sourced to Phase A synthesis
- **You'll need** — materials and environment
- **Before you start** — safety check and explicit exit permission ("You can stop at any step. Stopping is a win, not a failure.")
- **⚠ When NOT to use this** — contraindication flags, situational flags, relational flags
- **The practice** — numbered steps in plain language
- **Role-specific voice** (where applicable) — holding-parent voice and changing-parent voice
- **Signals to slow down or stop** — tailored to this specific protocol, not generic
- **Modifications** — shorter, lower-energy, or "hard day" versions
- **After** — optional 3-line reflection prompt, no scoring
- **Cross-references** — to Phase A synthesis and related protocols
- **Traditions honored** — explicit lineage attribution

## The principles that govern this kit

Drawn from Phase A synthesis §1:

1. **First, do no harm.** Safety first, always. When in doubt, the crisis response card applies.
2. **Honor all traditions.** Practices draw on many traditions (Jewish, Christian, Hindu, Buddhist, Islamic, Quaker, Indigenous philosophical orientation, secular humanist, Japanese aesthetics, and more). No single tradition is privileged as "the answer." Where a practice is drawn from a specific tradition, that tradition is named. You are free to set aside anything that does not resonate.
3. **Evidence with humility.** "May help," never "will cure." Every practice in this kit is offered; none is prescribed.
4. **Empower, don't control.** The walk belongs to the family. Nothing here is a verdict.
5. **Privacy as sanctuary.** This kit is designed to be used privately. The only document intended to leave the household is the one-page clinician handoff sheet.
6. **Accessible to all.** The protocols are written in plain language. Where technical terms appear, they are glossed.
7. **Continuous improvement.** Practices can be revised, set aside, or retired as the family's needs change. The "when things change" appendix describes how.

## How to decide what to try first

Read the triage one-page (`00-triage-one-page.md`) next. It will ask what the hottest pain is right now and map to one starting configuration and one starting practice. Begin with that one. One practice, for one week, done as consistently as possible. That is the entry level. When the practice feels like it fits, add a second. Do not try to do everything at once.

## If you are the mother reading this

Take what helps. Set down what doesn't. You know your walking in ways this kit cannot show. Nothing here is a verdict. Your instinct about what will work for your children is more accurate than this kit's instinct, which is why the practices are offered as possibilities and not prescriptions.

You are not alone. You are not without precedent. Humans across many cultures and many centuries have held families through what your family is facing. Some of what they learned is in this kit. Some of it is in the Phase A synthesis. And some of it will emerge from your own walking, which is yours to keep.

## If you are a clinician, therapist, or supervisor

The one-page handoff sheet (`00-clinician-handoff-sheet.md`) is written for you. It describes what the family may be practicing, how you can observe without disrupting, and how to feed back what is working or not working. The full research backing is in the Phase A synthesis if you want depth.
