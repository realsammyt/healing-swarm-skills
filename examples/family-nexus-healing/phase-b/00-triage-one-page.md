# Triage — One Page

*Read this after the how-to-use and crisis-response cards. It will help you pick ONE starting practice.*

---

## The principle

One practice. One week. One configuration.

Not six configurations at once. Not three practices in the first day. Not a comprehensive program you have to remember. **One practice, done as consistently as you can for one week, then check back and see how it feels.** This is how the entry level works for every configuration.

The reason is not modesty. The reason is that a family under acute strain has limited capacity to absorb new practices. Asking too much of yourselves is how practices get abandoned after two days and the kit gets set aside as "something that didn't work." Asking one small thing is how practices become actually-kept.

---

## The question

**What is the hottest pain right now?** Read the options below. Pick the one that matches. That tells you which configuration to start with.

### Option A: "Tomorrow is a supervised visit and I don't know how to make it okay."

**Start here:** `father-with-kids-supervised/entry/01-beginning-ritual-quiet-arrival.md`

This is the opening ritual for every supervised visit. It is small. It does not require the father to remember anything. It gives the visit a shape and a graceful way in. Once you and the children have practiced it, every visit has the same opening, and you have done one thing to make the visit more settled than it would otherwise be.

If tomorrow's visit is the hottest pain, read this protocol today, mentally rehearse it, and use it tomorrow. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option B: "The children are asking why Daddy can't come home, and I don't know what to say."

**Start here:** `mother-with-kids/processing-confusion/13-why-cant-daddy-live-with-us-script.md`

This protocol contains age-appropriate scripts for the 5-year-old and the 8-year-old. Said once, calmly, not repeated anxiously. The protocol also names what NOT to say — the over-promises that create more confusion later.

Read the protocol. Practice the scripts out loud once so they feel like your own words. Use them the next time the question comes. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option C: "I am holding everything and I am running on empty."

**Start here:** `mother-alone/container-practice/28-daily-five-minute-anchor.md`

This is the simplest daily practice for the mother in the kit. Five minutes, at the start of your day, that locates you before the day takes you. If you can do this consistently for one week, the container begins to hold.

Read the protocol. Choose the anchor that fits you (the protocol offers three options from different traditions — Jewish, Christian, Buddhist — or you can choose your own). Do it tomorrow morning, then the next morning. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option D: "The children are not sleeping."

**Start here:** `mother-with-kids/daily-regulation/11-bedtime-ritual-non-negotiable.md`

Bedtime ritual is the primary daily secure base for young children under stress. This protocol describes how to build (or rebuild) a bedtime that is boring, reliable, and non-negotiable even when everything else is chaotic. It is the single most evidence-supported intervention in the kit for children's regulation.

Read the protocol. Set the bedtime shape tonight. Keep it the same tomorrow and the next day. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option E: "I don't know if I am coping. I am worried about myself."

**Start here:** `00-crisis-response-card.md` if you have not already read it, and then: `mother-alone/re-centering/34-im-at-the-edge-escalation.md`

This is not a drill. The escalation protocol helps you notice where you are and name the path out before you are at the edge. If you have crossed the edge, the crisis-response card applies first — call 988, call your trusted contact, call your therapist.

If you are not at the edge but are worried, read both documents, identify your specific trusted contact, and write their number somewhere you can find it. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option F: "I am the father and I don't know what to do with myself."

**Start here:** `father-alone/presence-anchors/22-daily-dignity-rhythm.md`

This is the father's own daily practice. One recurring sensory anchor (sound, water, movement, breath), done the same way every day. It is not a therapy. It is a rhythm — something that is yours, that is consistent, that you can do even on harder days.

Read the protocol with a trusted adult if reading is becoming difficult. Choose one anchor that feels like it fits. Do it tomorrow, and the next day. That is the whole practice for this week.

### Option G: "The children just came back from a visit and they are not okay."

**Start here:** `mother-with-kids/grief-windows/15-emergency-co-regulation-90-seconds.md`

This is a 90-second family practice for the minutes right after a difficult visit, or any acute moment where things are hot. Whole family. Short. Body-based. No words required.

Read the protocol. Keep it in mind for today. Use it when the moment comes. That is the whole practice for this week.

## How to know it is working

Signs that the practice is fitting:

- You are doing it, even if imperfectly.
- The person you are doing it with is not being asked to be more than they are today.
- Something, however small, feels softer after than before.
- You want to do it again tomorrow.

Signs that the practice is NOT fitting:

- You are forgetting to do it, and forcing yourself to remember is adding stress.
- The person you are doing it with seems more tense, not less, during or after.
- You are performing it rather than meaning it.
- You feel worse after than before.

**If a practice is not fitting, it is not the right practice for this week. Set it down without apology.** Try a different option above. Or, for this week, do nothing new. Resting from the kit is a valid choice. The kit will be here next week.

---

## How to know it is time to add a second practice

Wait until the first practice has become something you do without thinking about. That usually takes longer than a week. Sometimes three weeks. Sometimes longer. **Middle level** in each configuration is where second and third practices live. The "when things change" appendix (`00-when-things-change.md`) describes the signals that the family is ready to deepen.

---

## If you are not sure

Start with whichever option above made you feel a small "yes" when you read it. That felt-sense is usually more accurate than analyzing. If no option made you feel a "yes," start with the one that made you feel the least "no."

If even that is too much: **put this kit down for today**. Read the crisis-response card if you have not. Come back tomorrow. The kit will wait.
