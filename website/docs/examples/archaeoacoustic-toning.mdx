# Archaeoacoustic Toning Example

A complete example demonstrating the Hyperhumanism & Sound Consciousness skills lifecycle for vocal toning at sacred site resonant frequencies.

## The Skill

| Property      | Value                                                              |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Name**      | `morning-resonance`                                                |
| **Purpose**   | 15-minute beginner archaeoacoustic toning at 110 Hz                |
| **Tradition** | Archaeoacoustics (Jahn & Dunne 1992, Cook et al. 2008, Smith 2023) |
| **Evidence**  | Preliminary (small-sample EEG/fMRI studies)                        |

## 5-Phase Structure

Based on Carl Hayden Smith's archaeoacoustic practice framework. Each phase builds on the previous:

| Phase         | Duration | Focus                                       |
| ------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1. Listen     | 60 sec   | Hear the target frequency (recorded tone)   |
| 2. Hum        | 90 sec   | Closed-mouth humming to match the frequency |
| 3. Vocal Tone | 90 sec   | Open-mouth toning at the target frequency   |
| 4. Harmonics  | 60 sec   | Explore overtones emerging naturally        |
| 5. Silence    | 30 sec   | Rest in resonance, notice after-effects     |

## Research Basis

> **Evidence level: Preliminary**
>
> Cook et al. (2008, n=30 fMRI) found 110 Hz tones shifted prefrontal cortex
> activity toward right-hemisphere dominance compared to control frequencies.
> Jahn & Dunne (1992, JASA) documented 117 Hz resonance in the King's Chamber
> at Giza. Smith et al. (2023, EVA London) documented 139.01 Hz resonance at
> Saqqara. All studies are small-sample and require replication.

## Safety Review: Passed

- Evidence honestly labeled as "Preliminary"
- Volume limit enforced (max 85 dB, living-room conversation level)
- Maximum 5 minutes per frequency segment
- Hearing condition screening included
- "Suggests" language throughout (never "proves")
- Grounding anchor before and after practice
- Not a substitute for audiological or medical care
