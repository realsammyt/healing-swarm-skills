# umwelt-practice

**Trigger:** `/umwelt-practice`

Sensory augmentation exercises for extending awareness of your own senses and imaginative exploration of other organisms' perceptual worlds. Based on von Uexkull's umwelt theory (1934) and Carl Hayden Smith's umwelt hacking concept. **CONTRAINDICATED for dissociative disorders.**

- **Umwelt Facilitator** - Practice design and dissociation safety
- **Content Writer** - Practice language and framing
- **Ethics Guardian** - Dissociation safety review (CRITICAL)

## Safety: Perceiving-as-Other (Level 3)

**CONTRAINDICATED for:**

- Active dissociative disorders (DID)
- Active depersonalization/derealization
- Active psychosis
- Severe PTSD with dissociative features

**Required safety features:**

- Full Tier 2 screening before Level 3
- Maximum 5-minute imagination segments
- Grounding between every segment
- "Imagine" language throughout (NEVER "become" or "you are")
- **Return-to-Default Protocol (MANDATORY)**

## Return-to-Default Protocol

1. Name 5 things you can see
2. Say your full name aloud
3. Touch a solid surface and describe its texture
4. State today's date and your location
5. Take 3 normal breaths before standing

## Research Basis

> **Evidence level: Established (umwelt theory) / Preliminary (practice)**
>
> Von Uexkull's umwelt theory (1934) is established biology. Embodied
> cognition research supports the idea that directed sensory attention
> shifts perceptual experience. Smith's umwelt hacking concept — deliberately
> shifting perceptual frames through practice — is a theoretical framework.
> Perceiving-as-other is an imagination exercise, not actual perceptual access.
