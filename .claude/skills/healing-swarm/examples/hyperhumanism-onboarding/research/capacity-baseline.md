# Capacity Baseline: Endo-Technology Literacy Assessment

> Initial baseline for a composite, fictional participant beginning a
> hyperhumanism onboarding. It reads their starting consciousness literacy
> across the four clusters in `shared/consciousness-literacy-levels.md`, so
> later reflection has a reference point. This is a self-report snapshot, not
> a psychometric measure.

---

## About This Baseline

The participant is a composite, fictional adult who spends most of a working
day on screens and wants to feel more present and more able to find their way
around without a phone in hand. Nothing here identifies a real person.

Carl Hayden Smith's framing is that practices are not separate one-off
interventions; over time they build consciousness literacy, the way learning
letters, then words, then whole texts builds reading literacy. This baseline
names where the participant starts in each cluster, using the observable
markers from `shared/consciousness-literacy-levels.md`.

> **A note on honesty:** The literacy levels are self-report anchors, not a
> validated scale. They have no norms and no diagnostic meaning, and they
> measure nothing about the participant's health or worth. A higher level names
> more practice, nothing more. This baseline does not replace medical or
> psychological care.

---

## Levels at a Glance

The four ordered labels, from `shared/consciousness-literacy-levels.md`:

| Level       | One-line marker                                                       |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Nascent     | Notices a state mostly afterward, needs full external structure       |
| Developing  | Recognises a state as it arises, enters a familiar practice with a cue |
| Established | Enters, sustains, and leaves a practiced state on purpose             |
| Embodied    | Portable in ordinary life, can be adapted or taught                   |

Levels are tracked separately per cluster, because skill in one does not
transfer automatically to another.

---

## Baseline by Cluster

### Somatic

**Self-reported level: Developing**

The participant does a short breathing practice a few mornings a week and can
put a plain name to feeling settled or unsettled in the body as it happens.
They lose the thread when the day gets busy. Breathwork is Well-established as
an endo-technology in `shared/endo-technology-taxonomy.md`; gentle coherent
breathing is broadly accessible, with Caution noted for acute anxiety.

### Perceptual

**Self-reported level: Nascent**

The participant notices sounds, light, and texture mostly after the fact,
usually when prompted. Sensory foregrounding is Preliminary in the taxonomy,
with a Caution for dissociation. This is new territory for them, and full
structure helps: a short guided cue to bring one sense forward at a time.

### Relational

**Self-reported level: Nascent**

Most of the participant's contact with others runs through messaging, which
they report as convenient and also a little flattening. They have no regular
shared or paired practice. Any relational work would start fully supported and
consent-first, and would not include a challenge dimension at this stage, since
that belongs only inside established trust.

### Attentional

**Self-reported level: Developing**

The participant can rest attention on their breath or a single object for a
short while and notices when it wanders, though returning takes effort. Silence
is Moderate in the taxonomy, with Caution for rumination-prone states; the
participant reports no such pattern, and short intervals suit them.

---

## Reading the Baseline

- Levels are uneven across clusters, which is expected. The participant is
  Developing in somatic and attentional work and Nascent in perceptual and
  relational work in the same week.
- The two Nascent clusters are simply newer, not weaker. They call for more
  structure and shorter durations at first.
- This is a starting page, not a score. Movement backward later, after a busy
  stretch or an illness, would be ordinary data, not a loss of worth.

The onboarding carries this baseline forward into the four-phase inquiry, where
the participant chooses one capacity to reconnect with directly.
