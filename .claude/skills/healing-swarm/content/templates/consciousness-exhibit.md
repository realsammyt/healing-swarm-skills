# Consciousness Exhibit Template

> Template for designing a Museum of Consciousness-style audio exhibit: a
> guided sequence of short consciousness samples with a Milliere 6D
> self-assessment between each. Covers the exhibit concept, the audio
> journey arc, the assessment integration, and visitor safety with a
> return-to-baseline re-entry. Serves the sound-consciousness skill.

---

[2-3 sentences naming the theme of this exhibit and what it invites the

> **Evidence note:** Each sample below draws on its own practice
> tradition with its own evidence base, noted per sample. Mindfulness,
> breath awareness, and body scan practices are well studied. Sound and
> silence practices have a moderate base. The Milliere 6D model is a
> phenomenological framework for describing dimensions of self-experience,
> used here for reflection rather than clinical measurement. Research
> suggests directed attention can shift the felt quality of experience.
> The exhibit records observations, not outcomes.

## Visitor Safety and Screening

**This exhibit is not appropriate right now if you have:**

- Active psychosis or psychotic features
- An active dissociative disorder
- Severe derealization or depersonalization
- A recent psychiatric hospitalization (within 3 months)

**Before you begin:**

- You may stop at any point, for any reason, with no explanation needed
- There is no experience you are supposed to have
- A grounding anchor is available between every sample
- A full return-to-baseline protocol closes the exhibit

_If you have a mental health condition, consult your provider before
beginning._

and the visitor always returns to solid ground before leaving.

## Milliere 6D Self-Assessment

Rate each dimension from 1 to 7. There is no right answer, and the number
is only a snapshot of this moment.

1. **Sense of agency:** How much do you feel in control of your actions
   right now? (1-7)
2. **Sense of ownership:** How much does your body feel like your body
   right now? (1-7)
3. **Self-location:** How clearly can you locate where you are in space?
   (1-7)
4. **First-person perspective:** How strongly do you experience the world
   from a single viewpoint? (1-7)
5. **Unity of experience:** How unified or fragmented does your experience
   feel? (1-7)
6. **Narrative self:** How present is your life story or personal
   narrative right now? (1-7)

## Element Guidelines

### Exhibit Concept

**Purpose:** Give the exhibit a clear, honest theme

**Key principles:**

- Frame it as an invitation to observe, not a treatment
- Name what the visitor is invited to notice
- Attribute the Museum of Consciousness and Carl Hayden Smith

### Audio Journey Arc

**Purpose:** Sequence samples safely from familiar to novel and back

**Key principles:**

- Order samples so attention settles before anything less familiar
- No sample exceeds 5 minutes
- A grounding anchor sits between every sample
- The journey ends grounded, never open-ended

### Sample Sections

**Purpose:** Guide each consciousness sample

**Key principles:**

- Evidence level stated at the top of each sample
- One instruction per step, body-anchored cues
- Grounding anchor after every sample
- 6D self-assessment after every sample
- No pressure toward a particular experience

### 6D Self-Assessment

**Purpose:** Offer language for dimensions of self-experience

**Required:**

- All six dimensions phrased as neutral, non-leading questions
- Baseline taken at the opening
- "No right answer" framing stated
- Used for reflection, not clinical measurement

### Re-Entry

**Purpose:** Return the visitor fully to baseline

**The return-to-baseline protocol is required and never abbreviated:**

- Feet and body weight
- Name five things you can see
- Say your full name
- State the date and location
- Three breaths before standing

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- Seated, standing, or lying down variations
- No movement required
- No equipment beyond audio playback

### Sensory Accessibility

- A text transcript accompanies the audio
- Visual and captioned alternatives for any spoken prompt
- Samples that lean on sound offer an attention-only variation

### Cognitive Accessibility

- Clear sample labels and predictable structure
- One instruction per step
- The 6D prompts use plain, concrete language

### Psychological Safety

- Dissociation screening before the exhibit
- Permission to stop at any sample
- Grounding anchor between every sample
- Return-to-baseline protocol at the close
- No pressure to report a specific experience
- Not a substitute for therapy

Before finalizing a consciousness exhibit:

- [ ] Exhibit concept stated and attributed to the Museum of Consciousness
- [ ] Dissociation screening present
- [ ] Samples ordered familiar to novel, then grounded
- [ ] No sample exceeds 5 minutes
- [ ] Grounding anchor between every sample
- [ ] Evidence level stated per sample
- [ ] 6D self-assessment after every sample, plus a baseline
- [ ] 6D prompts neutral and non-leading
- [ ] "No right answer" framing throughout
- [ ] Return-to-baseline protocol present and complete
- [ ] Comparison reflection included
- [ ] Text transcript and accessibility variations noted
- [ ] "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"
- [ ] Medical and mental health disclaimer present
