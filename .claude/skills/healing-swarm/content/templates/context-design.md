# Context Design Template

> Template for the context-design artifact: the experiential container that
> surrounds a practice. Covers physical setting, body priming, temporal
> framing, social setting, intention, and re-entry and integration. Based on
> Carl Hayden Smith's Contextology. Smith estimates that the conditions around
> an experience shape roughly 80% of its outcome; this artifact makes the
> container a deliberate design. Serves the context-engineering skill.

## Element Guidelines

### Intention

**Purpose:** Name why this practice and why now, as an invitation

**Key principles:**

- Held as an invitation, never as a demand or a program to complete
- Smith's open posture: capacity is something a person keeps growing into
- No language that implies the person is deficient or behind

### The Context Estimate

**Purpose:** Keep the headline claim honest

**Required:**

- Attribute the 80% figure to Smith every time it appears
- State plainly that it is his estimate, not an established research finding
- Use it as a reason to design carefully, not as a measured outcome claim

### Physical Setting

**Purpose:** Shape the room around the practice's intent

**Key principles:**

- Each choice named with the reason it fits the practice
- An adaptation column so the person can use their own space
- A boundary that gives the container a clear start and end

### Body Priming and Timing

**Purpose:** Design the state a person arrives in and the buffers around it

**Required:**

- A supported, sustainable posture
- An arrival buffer before the practice and a closing buffer after
- A closing buffer that never drops the person into a demanding task

### Re-entry and Integration

**Purpose:** Design the return, the most neglected part of any container

**Required:**

- A soft landing with grounding steps
- A protected return, free of jarring demand
- A stopping option available at any point

### Safety and Exclusions

**Purpose:** Keep the container safe and within bounds

**Required:**

- No substance-based setting (Smith's pharmaco-exclusion, absolute)
- No pressure to enter or continue an experience
- Contraindications of the inner practice carried into the container
- A clear note that this is preparation, not clinical care

## Accessibility Guidelines

Context design has real accessibility stakes. The setting itself can include or
exclude a person, so treat each dimension as a place to widen access rather than
narrow it.

### Sensory Accessibility

- **Light sensitivity:** Offer a low-light or eyes-closed option; avoid flicker
  and harsh overhead light; name a dimmable alternative
- **Sound sensitivity:** Offer a fully silent variation; when masking sound is
  used, keep it low and steady and let the person choose or remove it
- **Vision or hearing differences:** Provide the setting instructions in more
  than one form; do not rely on a single sense to establish the container

### Physical and Mobility Accessibility

- Every posture has a seated and a lying-down alternative
- No arrangement requires standing, floor-sitting, or movement the person cannot do
- The boundary and buffers work in a bed, a chair, or a wheelchair
- No equipment is required to establish the container

### Cognitive and Neurodivergent Accessibility

- Plain language and one instruction per step
- A predictable structure the person can rely on across sessions
- Sensory-load control (fewer inputs) for those who need it, and permission to
  add or remove elements of the setting freely
- No requirement to complete every dimension; a partial container is fine

### Psychological Safety

- Intention is an invitation, never a demand
- A stopping option is present and stated plainly
- The re-entry protects the person's return
- The container is private to the person and is not therapy

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a context design:

- [ ] All six dimensions present (setting, priming, timing, social, intention, re-entry)
- [ ] 80% context figure attributed to Smith and marked as his estimate
- [ ] Intention held as an invitation, not a demand, in Smith's open posture
- [ ] Physical setting choices each carry a reason and an adaptation
- [ ] Arrival buffer and closing buffer both present
- [ ] Re-entry design with a soft landing and a protected return
- [ ] Stopping option stated plainly
- [ ] Pharmaco-exclusion honored (no substance-based setting)
- [ ] Contraindications of the inner practice carried into the container
- [ ] Sensory adaptations for light and sound sensitivity
- [ ] Mobility adaptations (seated, lying, no-movement options)
- [ ] Cognitive and neurodivergent access (plain language, predictable structure)
- [ ] "Research suggests" language where any research is cited, no "science proves"
- [ ] Smith attributed for the Contextology framing
- [ ] Preparation-not-clinical-care note included
