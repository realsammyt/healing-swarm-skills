# Resonance Body Map Template

> Template for creating a body-mapping artifact for vocal toning practice.
> Records where resonance is felt in the body at each documented frequency
> and vowel, with a before and after session comparison. Serves the
> archaeoacoustic-toning skill. This is a self-observation record, not a
> diagnostic instrument.

## Safety Before Toning

- Warm up your voice with gentle humming before any sustained toning
- Keep the volume at a comfortable, conversational level, never strained
- Keep water within reach and sip between frequency segments
- Pause if you notice vocal strain, dizziness, or any ear discomfort
- An ear infection, acute tinnitus, or a vocal cord condition means you
  skip vocal production for now and use the listening-only variation
- This map is a note to yourself about sensation, not a health assessment

## Element Guidelines

### What This Map Is For

**Purpose:** Set the frame as self-observation, never diagnosis

**Key principles:**

- State plainly that little or no sensation is a valid result
- Keep the evidence note honest: documented acoustics, subjective felt
  sensation, no health claim
- Attribute Smith's archaeoacoustic research and the Museum of Consciousness

### Baseline and Comparison

**Purpose:** Let the practitioner see change against their own starting point

**Key principles:**

- Baseline is taken before any toning
- The same regions appear in baseline and comparison so the two line up
- "Shift?" is a yes or no observation, not a score to maximize

### Per-Frequency Maps

**Purpose:** Record resonance per documented frequency and vowel

**Key principles:**

- Frequency labeled with its site and its documented Hz value
- Vowel and self-selected pitch noted, never a prescribed exact pitch
- One map per frequency, filled after a few seconds of silence
- Free-text quality field so the practitioner uses their own language

### Evidence Framing

**Purpose:** Keep claims inside the documented record

**Required in every map:**

- Frequencies cited as acoustic measurements with a source
- "Research suggests" language, never "science proves"
- No claim that a frequency activates, heals, or produces a fixed state

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- Usable seated, standing, or lying down
- Listening-only variation for anyone who is not toning vocally
- No writing required; regions can be spoken aloud or held in memory

### Sensory Accessibility

- Large-print and screen-reader friendly table layout
- A verbal description alternative for each region
- Interoception varies widely, so faint or absent sensation is expected

### Cognitive Accessibility

- One region per row, one decision per row
- Predictable, repeated table structure
- Plain language throughout, no technical anatomy required

### Psychological Safety

- No sensation is framed as failure
- No pressure to produce a dramatic result
- The map is private and belongs to the practitioner
- Not a substitute for medical or audiological care
