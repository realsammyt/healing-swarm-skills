## Pre-Practice Safety Check

```
Before beginning, please confirm:

□ This is everyday emotional material (not trauma, grief, or crisis)
□ I am in a reasonably stable emotional state right now
□ I have time and space to ground afterward
□ I am willing to stop if it becomes too intense
□ This material is NOT being processed in current therapy

If any answer is no, consider waiting or working with a professional.
```

---

## Important Notice

```
This practice is a structured self-inquiry exercise, not psychotherapy.
It is designed for everyday emotional material — mild frustrations,
recurring patterns, projections onto others.

It is NOT appropriate for:
• Traumatic memories (abuse, assault, accidents)
• Active grief (recent death or loss)
• Suicidal or self-harm thoughts
• Material being processed in therapy

If you experience distress, stop the practice and contact:
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988)
```

## Structure

### Grounding (2-3 min)

```
Sit comfortably. Feel your feet on the floor.

Take three slow breaths.
With each exhale, let your shoulders drop slightly.

You are about to explore a quality or person that carries emotional charge.
You are in control of this process at every step.
You can stop at any time — stopping is wisdom, not failure.

Bring to mind [SHADOW FIGURE/QUALITY].
Notice what happens in your body as you think of them.
```

### Step 3: FACE IT — Third Person (4-7 min)

```
Describe [SHADOW FIGURE] as if telling a friend about them.
Use third-person language: he, she, it, they.

What are their qualities?
What do they do that bothers you?
What is it about them that gets under your skin?
What would the world be like if everyone were like them?

Notice your body as you describe them.
Where do you feel the charge — chest, jaw, stomach, hands?

Take your time. Be specific.
```

### Step 2: TALK TO IT — Second Person (4-7 min)

```
Now imagine [SHADOW FIGURE] is sitting across from you.
Speak directly to them. Use "you" language.

"You always..."
"What I want to say to you is..."
"What makes you act this way?"
"What are you trying to protect?"
"What do you need?"

Now pause. Listen.
If they could respond, what might they say?
Let the response come without forcing it.

Stay curious, not adversarial.
This is a conversation, not a confrontation.
```

### Step 1: BE IT — First Person (4-7 min)

```
Now take one more step.
Become [SHADOW FIGURE]. Speak as them. Use "I" language.

"I am..."
"I want..."
"I need..."
"The reason I act this way is..."

What does it feel like to be this quality?
Where in your body does this quality live?

Notice: what was "out there" is now "in here."
This quality is not alien — it is a disowned part of you.
It has something to offer, even if you have avoided it.
```

### Integration (2-3 min)

```
Gently return to your own perspective.

What surprised you?
What did you learn about yourself?
Where in your life might this quality actually serve you?

Place your hand on your chest if that feels comfortable.
"This is part of me. It is part of being human."

Take three grounding breaths.
Feel your feet on the floor.
Notice the room around you — colors, sounds, textures.

You may want to write a few notes about what you discovered.
```

## Variations

### Journal Version (Written)

Complete the 3-2-1 process in writing:

1. **Third person**: Free-write describing the figure
2. **Second person**: Write a letter to the figure
3. **First person**: Write a journal entry as the figure
4. **Integration**: Write what you learned

### Quick Version (5-7 min)

- One sentence per step
- Good for daily shadow hygiene
- "What charged me today? What would it say? What is it in me?"

### Paired Version (with partner)

- Partner reads prompts, practitioner responds
- Partner witnesses without interpreting
- Both debrief separately before sharing
- Consent framework required

## Post-Practice Notes

```
If this practice brought up strong emotions:
• That is normal and not a sign of doing it wrong
• Take extra time with grounding
• Be gentle with yourself for the rest of the day
• Consider journaling about what you found

If emotions feel overwhelming or persist:
• This is a sign the material may need professional support
• Contact a therapist or counselor
• 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988)
```

## Evidence and Attribution

> The 3-2-1 Process was developed by Ken Wilber, Terry Patten, Adam Leonard,
> and Marco Morelli as part of Integral Life Practice (2008). It synthesizes
> elements from Gestalt therapy's empty-chair technique (Perls), Internal
> Family Systems (Schwartz), Voice Dialogue (Stone & Stone), and Jungian
> shadow theory.
>
> The integrated protocol has not been evaluated in controlled clinical trials.
> Individual source techniques have varying levels of empirical support.
> General principles of approaching (rather than avoiding) difficult emotional
> material are supported across multiple therapeutic traditions.
