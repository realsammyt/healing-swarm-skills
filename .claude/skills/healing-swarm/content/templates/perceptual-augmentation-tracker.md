# Perceptual Augmentation Tracker Template

> Template for tracking a single perceptual-augmentation practice through Carl
> Hayden Smith's three-step arc: a novel perspective widens perception, and wider
> perception widens the capacity to be in the world. It bridges umwelt sensory
> work (umwelt-facilitator) and endo-technology stacks (holotechnica-architect),
> mapping a baseline inventory, the practice performed, the perceptual shift, and
> plain markers of capacity in daily life. The arc is Smith's model, not an
> established mechanism.

---

## What This Tracker Is For

[2-3 sentences. This tracker follows one practice through a single arc: the new

> **Evidence note:** The three-step arc is Carl Hayden Smith's model for how a
> novel perspective can widen perception and, in turn, widen a person's capacity
> to act and be in the world. It is a theoretical framework, not an established
> mechanism, and no step is guaranteed to follow from the one before. Where a
> specific practice has its own research (for example interoceptive attention or
> guided imagery), note that separately. Research suggests that directed
> attention can shift perceptual experience; the arc as a whole remains a lens
> for reflection.

> **Safety:** Keep a grounding option within reach and stop if you feel dizzy,
> disconnected, or distressed. If the practice involves perceiving-as-other or
> deep sensory shifting, follow the screening and return-to-default protocol in
> `content/templates/umwelt-exercise.md` first. This tracker does not replace
> medical or psychological care.

## Element Guidelines

### The Three-Step Arc

**Purpose:** Keep the tracker faithful to Smith's model without overclaiming

**Key principles:**

- Name the arc as Carl Hayden Smith's model, a theoretical framework, not an
  established mechanism
- Do not imply that each step must follow from the last; a session can stop at
  any step
- Present a session with no shift as valid data, never as a failed practice

### Baseline and Shift Inventories

**Purpose:** Let the person see change in perception, channel by channel

**Key principles:**

- Only the channels the practice works with are marked; blanks are fine
- The same channel vocabulary appears before and after so they can be compared
- Descriptive words, not scores, so nothing gets summed into a false total

### Capacity-in-World Markers

**Purpose:** Ground Smith's third step in observable daily-life signals

**Required:**

- Markers stay small, concrete, and checkable (noticed more detail, felt
  steadier), never mystical attainments or health claims
- Room for the person to add their own marker
- "None noticed" is an explicit, unpunished option

### Evidence and Safety Framing

**Purpose:** Keep claims honest and practice safe

**Required:**

- Smith and the three-step arc labeled as a theoretical framework
- "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"
- Grounding option named, and a pointer to the umwelt screening and
  return-to-default protocol for perceiving-as-other work
- Medical and psychological disclaimer present

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- No writing required; entries can be spoken aloud or held in mind
- Usable seated, standing, or lying down, in any setting
- No equipment needed

### Sensory Accessibility

- Skip any channel a person does not use; extend the ones they do
- Screen-reader friendly tables with short cells
- A spoken-response alternative for every checkbox and row

### Cognitive Accessibility

- One step at a time, in order, with a plain label on each
- Short prompts and predictable structure
- No requirement to complete every field or every session

### Psychological Safety

- A session with no shift is framed as valid data, not failure
- No comparison to other people and no capacity ranking
- Grounding available at every step, with an explicit permission to stop
- Not a clinical assessment and not therapy
