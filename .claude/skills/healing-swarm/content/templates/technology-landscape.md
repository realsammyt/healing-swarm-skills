# Technology Landscape Template

> Template for the hyperhumanism technology audit (inquiry phase 2). Maps a
> person's device and app landscape and, for each one, which innate
> capacity it displaces and which it augments. The frame is curious and
> even-handed: technology is a set of tradeoffs, not an enemy. Serves the
> hyperhumanism-inquiry skill.

---

# [Person or Focus]: Technology Landscape

## What This Audit Is For

[2-3 sentences. This audit looks at the devices and apps in your daily
life and asks what each one takes over and what each one gives back. Most
technology does both. The aim is a clear, honest map, not a verdict about
whether any tool is good or bad.]

> **Evidence note:** Hyperhumanism is a theoretical framework from Carl
> Hayden Smith. It invites attention to how tools change our relationship
> with innate capacities. Where research exists on a specific effect, for
> example how satellite navigation relates to spatial memory, it is noted.
> The framework as a whole is a lens for reflection, not a validated
> instrument, and this audit makes no claim that any device causes harm.
> Research suggests both displacement and augmentation can happen at once.

> **A note on boundaries:** This is a private reflection, not a clinical or
> diagnostic tool. It draws no conclusions about your health, and it does
> not replace medical or psychological care.

## How to Read Displace and Augment

Every tool sits somewhere on a balance.

- **Displaces:** The tool does a capacity for you, so you exercise it less
- **Augments:** The tool extends a capacity, so you can do more than before

Many tools do both to different capacities. A map app can displace your
sense of direction while augmenting your reach into unfamiliar places.
Naming both sides is the point; neither side is a judgment.

## Element Guidelines

### What This Audit Is For

**Purpose:** Frame the audit as an honest map, not a verdict

**Key principles:**

- State that most technology both displaces and augments
- No tool is labeled good or bad
- Attribute Smith and the hyperhumanism framework

### Displace and Augment

**Purpose:** Give the person a balanced way to read each tool

**Key principles:**

- Define both terms in plain language
- Show that one tool can displace and augment at once
- Keep the reflection column neutral, not confessional

### The Landscape Table

**Purpose:** Map the real tools of a daily life

**Key principles:**

- Start from the devices and apps that shape an ordinary day
- Name a displaced capacity and an augmented capacity for each
- Capacity language lines up with the phase 1 inventory
- Room to add tools the starter rows miss

### Framing Discipline

**Purpose:** Keep the audit free of anti-technology bias and shame

**Required:**

- Curious, even-handed tone throughout
- No claim that a device causes harm
- Reclamation framed as addition, not deletion or blame

### Evidence Framing

**Purpose:** Keep claims honest

**Required:**

- Hyperhumanism labeled as a theoretical framework
- Tool-specific research cited only where it exists
- "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- No writing required; rows can be spoken aloud or held in mind
- Usable in any position or setting
- No equipment needed beyond the tools being audited

### Sensory Accessibility

- Screen-reader friendly table layout
- A spoken-response alternative for each row
- Plain-language capacity names, room to rename them

### Cognitive Accessibility

- One tool per row, a short set of columns
- Predictable structure and brief prompts
- No requirement to audit every tool at once

### Psychological Safety

- No tool framed as good or bad
- No shame about technology use
- The audit is private to the person
- Not a clinical assessment and not therapy

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a technology landscape:

- [ ] Curious, even-handed framing, no shame, no anti-technology bias
- [ ] Displace and augment both defined and both used per tool
- [ ] Capacity language lines up with the phase 1 inventory

- [ ] "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"
