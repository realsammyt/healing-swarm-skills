# Spectrum Meditation Template

> Template for progressive three-body state-training meditation.

## Structure

### Opening — Grounding (2-3 min)

```
Settle into your posture — seated, lying, or however is comfortable.
Close your eyes or soften your gaze.

Take three slow breaths, each one a little deeper than the last.
Feel the weight of your body.
Feel the surface supporting you.

This practice moves through three realms of experience:
the physical, the subtle, and the spacious.
Each builds on the previous. There is no rush.
Whatever you experience — or don't experience — is exactly right.

You can return to your breath at any point.
You can open your eyes at any point.
You can stop at any point.
```

### Gross-Realm Practice (8-10 min)

```
Begin with the body — the physical, tangible, sensory world.

Notice the sensation of breathing.
The rise and fall of the chest or belly.
The air moving through the nostrils.

Now expand awareness to the whole body.
Sensations: warmth, coolness, pressure, tingling, stillness.
Weight: where gravity pulls you toward the earth.
Sound: whatever sounds are present, near and far.

Stay with what is concrete and physical.
This is the gross realm — the waking world of the senses.
It is the foundation for everything that follows.

[SILENCE: 2-3 minutes]

If your mind wanders, gently return to sensation.
Wandering is not failure — returning is the practice.
```

### Transition to Subtle

```
Now, while keeping body awareness in the background,
allow your attention to soften and open.

You are about to shift from the physical to the subtle —
from sensation to imagery, from body to inner light.

If nothing particular happens, that is completely fine.
Not everyone accesses the subtle realm the same way.
Stay curious without forcing.

If at any point this feels disorienting or uncomfortable,
return to feeling your breath and your body on the surface beneath you.
```

### Subtle-Realm Practice (6-8 min)

```
With eyes closed, notice whatever appears in the inner field.

Colors, shapes, patterns of light.
Images that arise spontaneously — not forced, not imagined.
A sense of spaciousness or luminosity.
Sounds or tones that seem to come from within.

If inner imagery is not your mode, notice:
Emotional textures — subtle feelings without clear cause.
Energetic sensations — buzzing, flowing, pulsing.
Dream-like impressions — fleeting and half-formed.

The subtle realm is the dreaming world —
richer than thought, lighter than body.

Hold it lightly. Let images come and go.

[SILENCE: 2-3 minutes]

EXIT RAMP: If this space feels destabilizing,
return to your breath. Feel your feet.
Name three things you can see if you open your eyes.
You are safe. You are here.
```

### Transition to Causal (Intermediate/Advanced Only)

```
If you feel settled and grounded in the subtle realm,
you may now explore what lies beneath both body and image.

This is the causal realm — formless, spacious, still.
It can feel like deep sleep while awake.
It can feel like vast emptiness.
It can feel like nothing at all.

All of these are normal. Spaciousness can feel unfamiliar.
If anxiety arises about the formlessness, that is common.
You can always return to the subtle or gross realm.
```

### Causal-Realm Practice (4-6 min, if included)

```
Let go of images. Let go of sensations.
Rest in the space between thoughts.

Not trying to think.
Not trying not to think.
Just... space.

If a thought arises, let it dissolve on its own.
If nothing arises, rest in that nothing.

This is the causal realm — the deep sleep of awareness.
Not unconscious, but conscious of spaciousness itself.

[SILENCE: 2-3 minutes]

If this feels overwhelming or vacant,
return to your body. Feel the surface beneath you.
This is not a test. There is no failure here.
```

### Nondual Pointer (1-2 min, advanced only)

```
One final question — not to answer, but to sit with:

The awareness that has been noticing body,
  noticing images,
  noticing spaciousness —

Is that awareness itself separate from what it notices?

Don't answer. Just... look.

[SILENCE: 30-60 seconds]

Whatever you found, or didn't find, is complete.
```

### Integration (2-3 min)

```
Gently allow the practice to settle.

Return to the body — the simplest, most grounding realm.
Feel your feet. Feel your hands.
Feel the breath moving.

Take three slow breaths.

Notice how you feel now compared to when you began.
Not better or worse — just noticing.

When you are ready, open your eyes.
Let the room come back gradually.

Take a moment before moving into activity.
```

## Pre/Trans Discernment Check

```
After practice, briefly reflect:

GENUINE TRANSCENDENCE markers:
• Greater calm, compassion, or perspective
• Feeling more connected, not less
• Humility about the experience
• "Nothing special happened" and that's okay

REGRESSION markers (seek support if persistent):
• Feeling special, superior, or chosen
• Dissociation — feeling disconnected from reality
• Grandiosity — "I've broken through"
• Persistent disturbance in daily functioning

If regression markers are present, practice grounding
and consider consulting a mental health professional.
```

## Variations

### Foundation Only (Gross Realm, 10-15 min)

- For beginners or as daily maintenance
- Body awareness and breath only
- Strong grounding foundation

### Gross + Subtle (20 min)

- Standard intermediate practice
- Stops before causal realm
- Most widely applicable

### Full Spectrum (25-30 min)

- All three realms + nondual pointer
- For experienced practitioners only
- Requires demonstrated stability in each realm

---

## Evidence and Attribution

> This practice draws on Ken Wilber's integral state training model (2006),
> which maps contemplative experience across gross, subtle, and causal realms
> — corresponding to the Vedantic three bodies. Focused attention meditation
> (gross realm) has strong research support (Lutz et al., 2008; Fox et al.,
> 2014). Open monitoring practices (subtle realm) have growing evidence
> (Colzato et al., 2012). Causal and nondual stages are drawn from
> contemplative tradition reports and theoretical frameworks rather than
> controlled empirical research.
