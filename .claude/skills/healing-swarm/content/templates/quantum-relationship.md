# Quantum-Relationship Template

> Template for designing a quantum relationship: a peer relationship that
> agrees to develop both people, not only comfort them. Based on Carl Hayden
> Smith's idea that some relationships accelerate us by challenging how we see
> the world. Safety is the point of this template. Challenge here is opt-in,
> built on established trust, held inside warmth, and revocable by either
> person at any time. This describes peer practice between equals, not therapy.

## Consent to the Challenge Dimension

Challenge is opt-in for both people, never assumed. Neither person offers
sharpening feedback until both have said yes to it, in plain words.

**Both peers confirm:**
☐ We both want this relationship to develop us, not only reassure us
☐ We each consent to being challenged, and to challenging, on purpose
☐ Either of us can withdraw that consent at any time, with no reason owed

Without both peers agreeing, the relationship stays supportive only, which
is a complete and healthy relationship in its own right.

---

## Warmth Before Challenge

Challenge only lands well inside trust that is already there. Warmth is not a
warm-up to get past; it is the ground the challenge stands on.

- Name the trust you have built before adding challenge: [What tells you this
  relationship is safe enough for honesty]
- Keep warmth the larger share: far more support than sharpening, always
- A challenge offered without warmth is criticism wearing a useful word
- When trust is thin or strained, return to support only until it steadies

---

## The Line Between Growth-Challenge and Harm

Growth-challenge stretches how someone sees; it never diminishes who they
are. The line is not subtle, and either person may call it at any moment.

| Growth-challenge (inside the agreement) | Outside it, always |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| A question that opens a fixed view      | A verdict about a person's worth |
| Feedback aimed at an idea or a pattern  | An attack aimed at the person |
| Offered once, then released             | Pushed until it lands |
| Stops the moment either person asks     | Ignoring a stop, a boundary, or a no |
| Held with warmth                        | Contempt, mockery, or intimidation |

Criticism, contempt, and boundary violation sit outside this agreement.
Naming a behaviour as challenge does not make it acceptable.

- The agreement can be renegotiated whenever it stops fitting either person
- Returning to support only is a respected choice, not a failure

## Element Guidelines

### What This Agreement Is For

**Purpose:** Frame the relationship as developmental and consensual, not as
license for harshness

**Key principles:**

- State plainly that challenge is opt-in and that support-only is complete
- Attribute the quantum relationships idea to Carl Hayden Smith
- Keep the frame peer to peer, between equals

### Consent to the Challenge Dimension

**Purpose:** Make challenge an explicit, mutual, revocable choice

**Required:**

- Both peers opt in, in plain words, before any challenge is offered
- Consent is revocable by either person at any moment, with no reason owed
- Support-only is named as a healthy default, not a lesser option

### Warmth Before Challenge

**Purpose:** Anchor challenge inside established trust

**Required:**

- Challenge lands only where trust is already built
- Warmth stays the larger share of the relationship at all times
- A thin-trust or strained state returns the pair to support only

### The Line Between Growth-Challenge and Harm

**Purpose:** Draw a clear boundary between challenge and abuse

**Required:**

- Growth-challenge aims at ideas and patterns, never at a person's worth
- Criticism, contempt, and boundary violation are named as outside the
  agreement, with no exceptions
- Either person can call the line at any time and be honoured

### Opt-Out and Renegotiation

**Purpose:** Keep an exit and a reset available to both people always

**Required:**

- A shared pause word either person can use
- "Not today" honoured without explanation
- Renegotiation and return to support only both framed as valid

### Not Therapy

**Purpose:** Keep the scope honest

**Required:**

- Named as peer practice, not therapy, coaching, or any form of care
- Does not replace medical or psychological support
- "If it feels like harm, stop" stated clearly

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- No writing required; the agreement can be spoken and agreed aloud
- Usable seated, standing, moving, or at a distance by call
- No equipment needed

### Sensory Accessibility

- Screen-reader friendly table and checklist layout
- A spoken alternative for each consent item and the pause word
- Plain-language terms throughout, with room to reword them together

### Cognitive Accessibility

- Short sections, one idea each
- The pause word gives a single, simple way to stop
- No requirement to complete the agreement in one sitting

### Psychological Safety

- Challenge is opt-in, warmth-anchored, and revocable at any moment
- A clear line between growth-challenge and harm, callable by either person
- Support-only framed as complete and healthy
- Not a clinical relationship and not therapy
