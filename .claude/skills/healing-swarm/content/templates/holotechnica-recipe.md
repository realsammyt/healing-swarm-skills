## Variations

### If this feels too long

- Remove one middle ingredient
- Reduce each ingredient by 1-2 minutes
- Keep preparation and integration — these are not optional

### If this feels too intense

- Replace [higher-intensity ingredient] with [gentler option]
- Extend transitions to 3 minutes with extra grounding
- Focus on body awareness between ingredients
- Stop at any point — partial stacks count

### To deepen the practice

- Extend ingredients to maximum recommended duration
- Add self-assessment (Milliere 6D) after each ingredient
- Maintain a trait-tracking journal over weeks
- Practice at the same time daily

### For different situations

- **Morning:** Energizing sequence — movement first, stillness last
- **Midday:** Reset — simplified 2-ingredient stack, 15 minutes
- **Evening:** Calming sequence — gentle, descending energy
- **Before creative work:** Flow stack — imagery and silence focus

## Building a Practice

**Frequency:** 3-5 times per week for trait development.
Daily is optimal. Consistency matters more than duration.

**Timeline:** Allow 2-4 weeks before evaluating. Trait changes
are gradual. State changes may be noticed sooner.

**Progression:**

- Week 1-2: Simple stack (2 ingredients, 15 min)
- Week 3-4: Standard stack (3-4 ingredients, 30 min)
- Month 2+: Extended stack if desired (5+ ingredients, 45 min)
- Ongoing: Refine ingredient selection based on experience

## Element Guidelines

### Contraindication Check

**Purpose:** Ensure safety through additive logic

**Requirements:**

- Every ingredient's contraindications listed individually
- Full union of all contraindications stated clearly
- Pharmaco-technology exclusion stated explicitly
- "If ANY applies, modify the stack" language
- Medical referral for contraindicated users

**This section must NEVER be abbreviated or made optional.**

### Preparation Phase

**Purpose:** Ground the practitioner before the stack

**Key principles:**

- Grounding in body and breath
- Intention setting (non-striving)
- Mental transition from daily activity
- Present in every stack regardless of depth

### Ingredient Sections

**Purpose:** Guide each endo-technology practice

**Key principles:**

- Evidence level stated at the top
- Purpose in the stack explained (why this ingredient, why here)
- Step-by-step instructions (one per step)
- Body awareness cues throughout
- Normalized possible experiences
- Permission to stop

### Transitions

**Purpose:** Ground between ingredients

**Key principles:**

- Mandatory for standard and extended depth
- Return to body awareness and breath
- Brief grounding (feet, weight, sounds)
- Frame as part of practice, not interruption
- Duration: 1-2 min (standard), 2 min (extended)

### Integration Phase

**Purpose:** Ground the full practice and support trait development

**Key principles:**

- Return to ordinary awareness
- Body awareness comparison (before vs. now)
- Emotional and mental state check
- Journaling opportunity (optional)
- Smooth transition to daily activity
- NEVER rushed or skipped

### Evidence Brief

**Purpose:** Honest per-ingredient and stack-level evidence

**Required in every recipe:**

- Per-ingredient evidence with study citations and sample sizes
- Limitations per ingredient
- Stacking methodology labeled as THEORETICAL
- Altered traits dosage context (Goleman & Davidson 2017)
- No outcome promises

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- Seated, standing, or lying down variations
- Movement ingredients have seated alternatives
- No equipment required
- Adaptations for limited mobility

### Cognitive Accessibility

- Clear ingredient labels and phase markers
- One instruction per step
- Predictable structure (same format every ingredient)
- Simple transitions

### Psychological Safety

- Permission to stop at any ingredient
- Permission to skip an ingredient
- No pressure for specific experiences
- Emotional release normalized
- Not a substitute for therapy
- Not a performance
