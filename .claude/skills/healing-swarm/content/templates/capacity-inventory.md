# Capacity Inventory Template

> Template for the hyperhumanism capacity inventory (inquiry phase 1). Maps
> which innate human capacities a person still exercises directly and which
> they have handed to devices or apps. The frame is curious, never
> anti-technology and never about shame. Serves the hyperhumanism-inquiry
> skill.

---

## Structure

```markdown
# [Person or Focus]: Capacity Inventory

**Framework:** Hyperhumanism (Carl Hayden Smith)
**Phase:** 1 of 4, Inventory
**Focus area:** [Optional, for example wonder, memory, presence]
**Date:** [Date]

---

## What This Inventory Is For

[2-3 sentences. This inventory is a snapshot of the innate capacities you
carry and how much you currently exercise each one. It is not a scorecard,
and delegating a capacity to a device is a normal, often useful choice.
The point is to see clearly, not to judge.]

> **Evidence note:** Hyperhumanism is a theoretical framework from Carl
> Hayden Smith that draws attention to innate human capacities and how
> technology changes our relationship with them. Where research exists on
> a specific capacity, for example the effects of awe experiences or of
> spatial memory, it is noted. The framework as a whole is a lens for
> reflection, not a validated instrument. Research suggests that regularly
> exercising a capacity tends to keep it available; this inventory helps
> you notice your own patterns.

> **A note on boundaries:** This is a private reflection, not a clinical or
> diagnostic tool. It measures nothing about your health or your worth, and
> it does not replace medical or psychological care.

---

## How to Use This

For each capacity, mark how much you exercise it directly, without a device
standing in for you. Use your honest sense of an ordinary week.

- **Direct:** You mostly do this yourself
- **Shared:** You do some yourself and hand some to a device
- **Delegated:** A device or app mostly does this for you

There is no ideal column to land in. A delegated capacity is not a failing.
```

## Element Guidelines

### What This Inventory Is For

**Purpose:** Frame the inventory as clear seeing, not scoring

**Key principles:**

- State plainly that delegating a capacity is normal and often useful
- No column is the correct answer
- Attribute Smith and the hyperhumanism framework

### The Inventory Table

**Purpose:** Let the person map each capacity honestly

**Key principles:**

- Direct, Shared, Delegated as neutral descriptions, not grades
- A free-text column for what stands in for a delegated capacity
- Room to add capacities the standard list misses
- Capacity set drawn from the hyperhumanism inquiry, including navigation,
  memory, attention, sensing, and social connection

### Framing Discipline

**Purpose:** Keep the inventory free of anti-technology bias and shame

**Required:**

- Curious tone, never judgmental about technology use
- No language that implies delegation is decline or failure
- Technology framed as a choice with tradeoffs, not an enemy

### Evidence Framing

**Purpose:** Keep claims honest

**Required:**

- Hyperhumanism labeled as a theoretical framework
- Capacity-specific research cited only where it exists
- "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- No writing required; rows can be spoken aloud or held in mind
- Usable in any position or setting
- No equipment needed

### Sensory Accessibility

- Screen-reader friendly table layout
- A spoken-response alternative for each row
- Plain-language capacity names with room to rename them

### Cognitive Accessibility

- One capacity per row, three plain options
- Predictable structure, short prompts
- No requirement to complete every row in one sitting

### Psychological Safety

- No column framed as good or bad
- No shame about technology use
- The inventory is private to the person
- Not a clinical assessment and not therapy

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a capacity inventory:

- [ ] Curious framing, not anti-technology, no shame
- [ ] Direct, Shared, Delegated presented as neutral descriptions
- [ ] Capacity set includes navigation, memory, attention, sensing,
      and social connection
- [ ] Room to add personal capacities
- [ ] Free-text column for what stands in for a delegated capacity
- [ ] Hyperhumanism labeled as a theoretical framework
- [ ] "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"
- [ ] Reflection prompts open, not leading
- [ ] A clear handoff to the phase 2 audit
- [ ] Accessibility variations noted
- [ ] Smith attributed
