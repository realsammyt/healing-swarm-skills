# Ecology-Design Template

> Template for designing a personal ecology of practices: a small, varied set
> of inner practices that stays resilient because no single one carries the
> whole system. Based on Carl Hayden Smith's principle that by generating an
> ecology we build in resilience through diversity. It assembles at least
> three complementary modalities, spanning somatic, perceptual, and
> relational work, and sets a rhythm for keeping the mix alive.

## Diversity Rules

- No single practice carries the whole system. If one modality falls away, the
  others still hold.
- Complementarity over redundancy: choose practices that do different work,
  not three versions of the same thing.
- Each modality earns its place by contributing something the others do not.
- One well-practised ingredient per domain beats several half-held ones.

---

## Rotation and Adaptation Cadence

An ecology stays alive by changing, not by staying fixed.

- **Review rhythm:** on a set rhythm, for example every 4 to 6 weeks, sit with
  the whole mix and read it as one system
- **Swap what has gone stale:** if a practice has become rote, rest it or
  replace it with another from the taxonomy in the same domain
- **Integrate what practice reveals:** let what you notice reshape the mix, and
  move an ingredient toward unaided form using the Scaffold→Remove
  Progression in shared/practice-pathways.md once a tool has done its work
- **Track literacy, not achievement:** note your level per cluster from
  shared/consciousness-literacy-levels.md as a trend over time, never a score

This design fits inside the Hyperhuman Cultivation Arc in
shared/practice-pathways.md, at its ecology-assembly stage.

## Element Guidelines

### What This Design Is For

**Purpose:** Frame the ecology as resilience through diversity

**Key principles:**

- State that no single practice carries the whole system
- Attribute the ecology-of-practices idea to Carl Hayden Smith
- Keep the scope to inner practices, with no substances

### Minimum Viable Ecology

**Purpose:** Set the floor at three complementary modalities

**Required:**

- At least three practices, spanning somatic, perceptual, and relational
- Every ingredient drawn from shared/endo-technology-taxonomy.md
- Domain names aligned with the clusters in
  shared/consciousness-literacy-levels.md

### Diversity Rules

**Purpose:** Keep the mix genuinely varied

**Key principles:**

- Complementarity over redundancy, so each modality does different work
- No practice load-bearing for the whole system on its own
- Depth per ingredient over a long, shallow list

### Rotation and Adaptation Cadence

**Purpose:** Keep the ecology alive rather than fixed

**Required:**

- A named review rhythm for reading the whole mix
- Permission to rest or swap a stale practice for another in the same domain
- A link to the Scaffold→Remove Progression for moving a tool-supported
  ingredient toward unaided form
- Literacy tracked per cluster as a trend, never scored

### Evidence Framing

**Purpose:** Keep claims honest

**Required:**

- Each ingredient's evidence level copied verbatim from the taxonomy
- Any combination labelled Theoretical as a whole
- "Research suggests" language, no "science proves"

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Physical Accessibility

- Practices can be chosen to fit any body and setting
- Seated, short, and low-effort options available in every domain
- No equipment required beyond the practices themselves

### Sensory Accessibility

- Screen-reader friendly table layout
- Plain-language domain and practice names, with room to reword them
- A spoken alternative for each design step

### Cognitive Accessibility

- One domain per row, a short set of columns
- Predictable structure and brief prompts
- No requirement to design the whole ecology in one sitting

### Psychological Safety

- Ingredient contraindications screened before beginning
- No practice framed as mandatory; the mix flexes to the person
- Review framed as tending, not as a test to pass
- Not a clinical plan and not therapy
