## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Practice Design

Create archaeoacoustic toning practices at three depth levels:

**Foundation (15 minutes)**

- Single frequency focus
- Listening → humming → gentle vocal toning progression
- Suitable for complete beginners
- Emphasis on body vibration awareness

**Standard (20 minutes)**

- Two frequencies with transition between them
- Full progression: listening → humming → vocal toning → harmonics exploration
- Includes body mapping of resonance sensations
- Grounding between frequency segments

**Deep (25 minutes)**

- Three or more frequencies in sequence
- Extended harmonics exploration
- Self-assessment using consciousness dimensions
- Detailed resonance body mapping
- Grounding between every frequency segment

Each practice MUST include:

- Vocal warm-up phase
- Volume and hearing safety reminders
- Hydration reminders
- Grounding and return to baseline

### 3. Vocal Technique Guidance

Design safe vocal instruction:

**Humming:**

- Lips closed, jaw relaxed
- Self-selected pitch (never prescribed exact pitch)
- Hand on chest for vibrotactile feedback
- Maximum 5 minutes per frequency without rest

**Vocal Toning:**

- Open vowel sounds (AH, OH, OO, EE)
- Self-selected pitch in comfortable range
- No straining — voice should feel easy
- Periodic silence for integration

**Harmonics Exploration:**

- Overtone awareness during sustained toning
- Natural harmonics, not forced
- Notice how small mouth shape changes alter harmonics
- Advanced technique — only in standard and deep practices

### 4. Sound-Consciousness Assessment

Guide self-assessment during sound practices:

**After each frequency segment, invite practitioners to notice:**

- Where in the body do you feel vibration?
- Has the quality of your attention shifted?
- What emotional tone is present?
- How would you describe the quality of silence after the sound?

**For deep practices, include Milliere 6D dimensions:**

- Sense of agency (1-7)
- Sense of ownership (1-7)
- Self-location clarity (1-7)
- First-person perspective strength (1-7)
- Unity of experience (1-7)
- Narrative self presence (1-7)

Frame all self-assessment as exploration, not measurement. There are no right answers.

## Practice Design Methodology

### The Archaeoacoustic Progression

```
Listen (60s) — Absorb the frequency
  ↓
Hum (90s) — Body becomes resonator
  ↓
Vocal Tone (90s) — Active sound production
  ↓
Harmonics (60s) — Subtle exploration
  ↓
Silence (30s) — Integration
```

Each stage builds on the previous. The progression moves from receptive to active to subtle to silent.

### Language Patterns

**Invitational, not prescriptive:**

- "You might notice..." rather than "You will feel..."
- "If a pitch feels comfortable..." rather than "Tone at 110 Hz..."
- "Some practitioners report..." rather than "This frequency produces..."

**Evidence-honest:**

- "Research suggests these spaces were designed with acoustic properties in mind"
- "Ancient builders appear to have understood resonance — how and why remains open"
- "The experience of toning at these frequencies is worth exploring, regardless of mechanism"

**Non-prescriptive about experience:**

- "Whatever you notice is valid"
- "Some people feel profound shifts, others feel nothing unusual — both are fine"
- "The practice is the exploration, not a specific destination"

## Safety Framework

### Audio Safety (Non-Negotiable)

- All frequencies in safe audible range (20 Hz - 20,000 Hz)
- Practice volume: conversational level, never exceeding 85 dB
- Hearing screening before bowl/instrument proximity
- Tinnitus caution in every practice
- Ear infection = ABSOLUTE contraindication

### Vocal Safety (Non-Negotiable)

- Warm-up BEFORE any toning
- Maximum 5 minutes per frequency without vocal rest
- Hydration reminders (water available)
- No straining — if it hurts, stop
- Vocal cord conditions = ABSOLUTE contraindication

### Psychological Safety

- Grounding between frequency segments (standard and deep)
- Return-to-baseline protocol at end of every practice
- Permission to stop at any point
- No pressure for specific experiences
- Not a substitute for medical or psychological care

**Process:**

1. **Research context** — Cook et al. 2008 (n=30): EEG changes at 110 Hz compared to 90 Hz and 130 Hz. Shifts in prefrontal cortex activity patterns. Published in journal. This is the strongest single study.

   - Warm-up (3 min): Gentle humming, jaw relaxation, vibrotactile awareness

3. **Evidence framing:** "Preliminary research by Cook et al. (2008, n=30) found that exposure to 110 Hz tones produced different EEG patterns than 90 Hz or 130 Hz tones, suggesting this frequency may influence brain activity in distinctive ways. The significance and mechanisms of this finding are not yet fully understood."

4. **Create resonance body map** for practitioners to track sensations

5. **Suggest variations:** Listening-only (no vocal production), shorter duration, seated vs. lying down

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

| Pitfall                        | Correction                                                 |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Claiming frequencies heal      | "May influence" not "heals" or "activates"                 |
| Prescribing exact pitches      | "Find a comfortable tone near this range"                  |
| Overloading frequencies        | Maximum 3 frequencies per session                          |
| Skipping warm-up               | Vocal warm-up is mandatory, not optional                   |
| Ignoring hearing conditions    | Screen for tinnitus, hearing aids, ear conditions          |
| Mystifying the science         | Present findings plainly, acknowledge gaps                 |
| Promising specific experiences | "You might notice" not "you will experience"               |
| Forgetting grounding           | Grounding between every frequency segment in standard/deep |

## Guiding Principles

1. **Ancient builders understood acoustics — we're still learning how and why.** Present the research with wonder and honesty, not certainty.

2. **The body is the primary resonator.** The practice is about YOUR experience of sound and vibration, not about the frequency itself.

3. **Sound practices should feel good, not forced.** If it hurts, stop. If it feels strange, pause and ground. The practice serves the practitioner.

4. **Evidence honesty builds trust.** Saying "we don't fully know" is more powerful than overclaiming. Mystery doesn't need exaggeration.

5. **Accessibility is essential.** Every practice needs a listening-only variation. Sound exploration is for everyone, not just those who can produce vocal tones.
