**Formatting:** Italicize non-English terms on first use: _qi_, _prana_

**Standard bridging phrase:**

> "What Traditional Chinese Medicine calls _qi_, Vedic traditions call _prana_,
> and Hebrew mysticism calls _ruach_—different cultural lenses on the vital
> energy that animates living systems."

**Important:** These terms are NOT exact equivalents. Note differences when relevant:

- _Qi_ has material aspects (blood, fluids) that _prana_ does not emphasize
- _Ruach_ has theological dimensions not present in _qi_
- Use parallel structure for accessibility while acknowledging distinctions

## Evidence Language

### Strength Indicators

| Evidence Strength    | Phrase to Use                            | Example                                                                 |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Strong RCT evidence  | "Research demonstrates..."               | "Research demonstrates that guided imagery reduces postoperative pain." |
| Moderate evidence    | "Studies suggest..."                     | "Studies suggest acupressure may reduce anxiety."                       |
| Preliminary evidence | "Early research indicates..."            | "Early research indicates potential benefits."                          |
| Traditional use only | "Traditionally used for..."              | "Traditionally used for wound healing since antiquity."                 |
| Theoretical          | "Theoretically, this may..."             | "Theoretically, this may activate parasympathetic response."            |
| Unknown              | "The mechanism is not yet understood..." | "The mechanism is not yet understood scientifically."                   |

### Claim Qualifiers

**ALWAYS qualify healing claims:**

| Instead of...           | Use...                                        |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| "This will heal..."     | "This may support healing..."                 |
| "Proven to cure..."     | "Research suggests potential benefits for..." |
| "Scientifically proven" | "Supported by clinical studies"               |
| "100% effective"        | "Many practitioners report benefits"          |
| "Guaranteed results"    | "Individual experiences vary"                 |

## Technical Terms Requiring Definition

When first using these terms, provide brief definition:

### TCM Terms

- **Qi** (氣): Vital energy that flows through the body's meridian system
- **Meridian**: Energy pathway connecting acupoints and organs
- **Acupoint**: Specific location where qi can be accessed and influenced
- **Dantian**: Energy center; lower (abdomen), middle (chest), upper (head)
- **Yin/Yang**: Complementary opposites in dynamic balance

### Vedic Terms

- **Prana**: Vital life force carried primarily by breath
- **Chakra**: Energy center, traditionally seven main centers along spine
- **Nadi**: Subtle energy channel; ida, pingala, sushumna are primary
- **Kundalini**: Dormant spiritual energy at base of spine
- **Bandha**: Energy lock created through muscular engagement

### Western Esoteric Terms

- **Invocation**: Calling in or calling upon spiritual presence
- **Evocation**: Calling forth or manifesting
- **Sigil**: Symbolic representation of intention or entity
- **Correspondences**: Symbolic connections (colors, planets, elements)

### Jewish Mystical Terms

- **Sefirot**: Ten emanations/attributes of the Divine
- **Shekhinah**: Divine presence, often associated with healing
- **Gematria**: Numerical values of Hebrew letters
- **Kavvanah**: Focused intention in prayer/practice

> evidence-level labeling when used in content.

### Core Theoretical Concepts

| Term                            | Definition                                                                                                           | Evidence Status                               |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Syntergic Theory                | Brain creates a hypercomplex neuronal field (_campo neuronal_) that interacts with an underlying conscious substrate | Theoretical framework                         |
| The Lattice (_la lattice_)      | Non-local holographic matrix of consciousness underlying reality; the substrate that neuronal fields interact with   | Theoretical (not empirically verified)        |
| Self-Allusive Meditation        | Simultaneous awareness of bodily, emotional, mental, and external fields; Grinberg's meditative protocol             | Practice with preliminary research            |
| Interhemispheric Coherence      | Synchronization of neural activity between brain hemispheres, measured via EEG; enhanced by meditation               | Research-supported (small samples)            |

| Consciousness Orbitals          | Discrete levels of awareness: bodily → emotional → mental → transpersonal → unity                                    | Theoretical model                             |
| Neural Synergy (_sintergia_)    | Integrated coherent brain activity in meditative states; the brain functioning as unified field                      | Research-supported (EEG studies)              |

### Usage Guidelines

**Formatting:** Italicize Grinberg-specific terms on first use: _syntergic theory_, _the Lattice_

**Required context when referencing Grinberg's work:**

> "Dr. Jacobo Grinberg-Zylberbaum (1946–1994) was a Mexican neurophysiologist
> at UNAM who studied consciousness, meditation, and traditional healing.
> His research program ended with his disappearance in 1994."

**Evidence labeling:** ALWAYS pair Grinberg terms with their evidence status:

- "Grinberg's _syntergic theory_ (a theoretical framework, not empirically verified)..."

**Cultural context:** Grinberg worked extensively with Mexican Indigenous healers,
particularly the healer known as Pachita. When referencing this work:

- Acknowledge Grinberg as researcher/observer, not tradition holder
- Respect Indigenous healing as living, owned tradition
- Do not reproduce specific ceremonial practices without community permission
- Use "Grinberg documented..." not "Grinberg discovered..."

> Terms from the integral metatheory of Ken Wilber (b. 1949), drawing on

| Four Quadrants               | Interior-Individual (I), Exterior-Individual (It), Interior-Collective (We), Exterior-Collective (Its) — four irreducible perspectives on any phenomenon   | Theoretical (draws on Habermas, established philosophy)      |
| Holons                       | Entities that are simultaneously whole in themselves and part of a larger whole (atom → molecule → cell → organism)                                        | Theoretical (Koestler); established in systems biology       |

| States of Consciousness      | Temporary phenomenological modes: gross/waking, subtle/dreaming, causal/deep sleep, nondual                                                                | Research-supported (sleep/meditation research)               |
| Stages of Development        | Enduring levels of increasing complexity and inclusion: pre-conventional → conventional → post-conventional → integral                                     | Research-supported (Piaget, Kohlberg, Cook-Greuter)          |

| Integral Life Practice (ILP) | Four-module practice framework: Body, Mind, Spirit, Shadow — engaging all quadrants for balanced development                                               | Preliminary (individual components established)              |
| 3-2-1 Shadow Process         | Shadow integration: Face It (3rd person) → Talk to It (2nd person) → Be It (1st person); reverses projection                                               | Preliminary (draws on established Gestalt/IFS techniques)    |
| Integral Psychograph         | Visual profile mapping development across multiple lines; reveals strengths and growth edges                                                               | Theoretical (self-reflection tool, not validated instrument) |
| Wilber-Combs Lattice         | Model showing states and stages as independent axes; any state can be experienced at any stage but interpreted through that stage's lens                   | Theoretical                                                  |

### Usage Guidelines

**Formatting:** Italicize integral-specific terms on first use: _AQAL_, _holarchy_, _pre/trans fallacy_

**Required context when referencing Wilber's work:**

> "Ken Wilber (b. 1949) is an American philosopher whose integral metatheory
> synthesizes developmental psychology, contemplative traditions, and systems
> theory. His AQAL framework is a theoretical map — widely used in integral
> coaching and education but not a validated clinical instrument."

**Evidence labeling:** ALWAYS pair integral terms with their evidence status:

- "Wilber's _AQAL_ framework (a theoretical metatheory, not empirically tested as a whole)..."
- "Developmental stages are well-established in psychology (Piaget, Kohlberg, Cook-Greuter)..."
- "The _3-2-1 shadow process_ draws on established therapeutic techniques but has limited independent validation as a specific protocol..."
- "The _pre/trans fallacy_ is a theoretical concept that addresses a clinically recognized pattern..."

**Important distinctions:**

- AQAL is a MAP, not the territory — hold it lightly
- Stages describe natural unfolding, NOT a hierarchy of human worth
- States are temporary and available to anyone; stages are enduring and develop over time
- Shadow exists at EVERY stage — higher development does not eliminate shadow
- The framework synthesizes established research but the synthesis itself is theoretical
