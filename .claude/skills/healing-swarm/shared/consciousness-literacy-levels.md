# Consciousness-Literacy Levels

> A shared four-level taxonomy for describing how skilled a person is at
> noticing, entering, sustaining, and leaving their own states, tracked across
> the modality clusters the swarm already uses. It describes skill at observing
> and working with one's own experience, not spiritual rank. There is no finish
> line and no one to outrank.

---

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Canonical taxonomy. When an agent, template, or the
`/consciousness-audit` check-in needs to name a literacy level, this file is the
source of record. The levels are self-report anchors, not a validated scale.

## What This Is

Carl Hayden Smith's culminating point in his consciousness work is that
practices are not separate one-off interventions. Over time they build
_consciousness literacy_, the way learning letters, then words, then whole texts
builds reading literacy. You do not finish reading; you get more fluent. Smith's
analogy is the frame for this file.

Literacy here means one plain thing: your skill at working with your own states.
Can you notice a state while it is happening? Can you enter a practiced state on
purpose? Can you stay with it, leave it, and carry something from it into an
ordinary day? These are observable skills a person can report on, not attainments
that make one person worth more than another.

> **A note on honesty:** This is a self-report reflection tool, not a
> psychometric instrument. The levels have no published norms, no cutoff scores,
> and no diagnostic meaning. They measure nothing about a person's health or
> worth. A higher level is not better as a person; it names more practice, that
> is all. This taxonomy does not replace medical or psychological care.

## The Four Clusters

Literacy is tracked separately in four clusters, because skill in one does not
transfer automatically to another. The clusters reuse the vocabulary already
established in `shared/endo-technology-taxonomy.md` and the Hyperhuman
Cultivation Arc in `shared/practice-pathways.md`. A person can sit at different
levels in different clusters, and that is expected.

| Cluster       | What it covers                                              | Example endo-technologies                    | Related skills / agents                          |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Somatic       | Working with breath, body sensation, and movement           | Breathwork, body scan, movement              | `/whm-breathwork`, `/somatic-practice`; somatic-guide, breathwork-coach |
| Perceptual    | Foregrounding a sense, extending the sensorium, sound        | Sensory foregrounding, visualization, sound / toning | `/umwelt-practice`, `/sound-healing`; umwelt-facilitator, sound-healing-guide |
| Relational    | Practising with another person or a group, with consent      | Paired resonance, group perception           | `/relational-practice`, `/resonance-pairing`; resonance-facilitator, community-facilitator |
| Attentional   | Sustaining and releasing attention; contemplative stillness  | Silence, meditation, coherence               | `/contemplative-inquiry`, `/coherence-meditation`; contemplative-guide, coherence-guide |

The Attentional cluster is sometimes written "attentional / contemplative" when
the setting is contemplative. It is one cluster.

## The Four Levels

The same four ordered labels apply inside every cluster. They are ordered, not
scored: nascent comes before developing, but the distance between them is not a
number and does not add up.

| Level       | One-line marker                                                                 |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Nascent     | Notices a state mostly afterward, and needs full external structure to enter it |
| Developing  | Recognises a state as it arises and can enter a familiar practice with a cue    |
| Established | Enters, sustains, and leaves a practiced state on purpose in a supportive setting |
| Embodied    | The capacity is portable, available in ordinary life, and can be adapted or taught |

## Level Detail

### Nascent

The starting point in a cluster. The practice is new, and most of the noticing
happens after the fact.

**Observable markers:**

- Can tell that something shifted, but usually only once the practice is over
- Relies on a guide, a recording, or step-by-step instructions to begin
- Loses the thread quickly when the external structure stops
- Finds it hard to name what a state feels like while it is happening

**Typical supports needed:**

- Fully guided sessions (audio, script, or a facilitator)
- A set container: same time, same place, clear start and end
- Short durations, with no expectation of sustaining anything

**Graduation to developing looks like:** the person can name a state _while it is
happening_ at least sometimes, not only in hindsight.

### Developing

The practice is becoming familiar. Recognition starts to happen in real time.

**Observable markers:**

- Recognises a state as it arises and can put a plain name to it
- Enters a familiar practice from a short cue or reminder, without a full script
- Stays with a state for a while before attention scatters
- Starts to notice what helps and what pulls them out

**Typical supports needed:**

- Light prompts rather than full guidance: a cue word, a timer, a checklist
- A reliable container, though it can flex more than at nascent
- Encouragement that drifting and returning is the practice, not a failure

**Graduation to established looks like:** the person can enter, hold, and leave
the state on purpose in a supportive setting, without step-by-step guidance.

### Established

The skill is dependable inside a supportive setting. The person is now working
the practice, not just following it.

**Observable markers:**

- Enters a practiced state deliberately, sustains it, and leaves it cleanly
- Notices when attention drifts and returns without being told
- Adjusts the practice in the moment (shortens, softens, changes anchor)
- Can describe the texture of a state clearly enough for someone else to follow

**Typical supports needed:**

- Little or no external cue during practice
- Occasional check-ins to compare notes and refine
- A setting that is supportive, though it no longer has to be perfect

**Graduation to embodied looks like:** the capacity shows up unprompted in daily
life, across settings, not only during a set practice.

### Embodied

The capacity is portable. It lives in ordinary life, not only on the cushion or
in the session. Embodied is not a summit or a rank; it names integration, and it
can still wobble.

**Observable markers:**

- Draws on the capacity in everyday moments, without a formal practice
- Adapts it to new settings, including hard or busy ones
- Recovers it after a disruption (illness, stress, a long gap) rather than
  starting from zero
- Can hold space for someone else learning the same skill, if they choose to

**Typical supports needed:**

- No external scaffold required for the capacity itself
- Rest, variety, and honest reflection to keep it alive rather than rote
- A community of practice, if the person wants one

**There is no level above embodied.** Fluency deepens without end, but the
taxonomy stops here on purpose, so no one treats literacy as a ladder to worth.

## Using These Levels

1. Rate each cluster separately, against the observable markers above, not
   against a felt sense of how advanced you are.
2. Ask "what can I notice and do", never "how special do I feel". The markers
   are behaviours, not experiences to chase.
3. Expect uneven levels across clusters. Someone can be established in somatic
   work and nascent in relational work in the same week.
4. Treat movement backward as ordinary data. A life disruption can move a
   cluster from established back to developing, and that is not a loss of worth.
5. Read the history as a trend per cluster over weeks and months, which is what
   the `/consciousness-audit` check-in and the longitudinal protocol in
   `shared/outcome-measurement.md` are for.
6. Never tie a level to a promise. These levels describe practice skill; they do
   not predict any health outcome and do not certify anything.
