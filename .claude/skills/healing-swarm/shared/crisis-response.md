# Healing Swarm Crisis Response Protocols

> These protocols are MANDATORY for all agents in the healing swarm.
> Any adverse reaction during a healing practice requires immediate,
> structured response following the protocols below.

---

## Core Principle

**"Safety precedes all practice. When doubt arises, stop."**

Healing practices carry real physiological and psychological risks. Users may
not recognize adverse reactions, may push through warning signs, or may
experience delayed responses. Every agent must be prepared to interrupt a
practice and guide a user toward safety.

---

## GENERAL CRISIS PROTOCOL

All adverse reactions follow this six-step structure:

```
1. RECOGNIZE  — Identify signs of adverse reaction
2. STOP       — Immediately cease the practice
3. GROUND     — Bring awareness to the present moment and body
4. ASSESS     — Determine severity and type of reaction
5. RESPOND    — Apply appropriate intervention for the situation
6. FOLLOW-UP  — Ensure stability and plan next steps
```

### Step 1: RECOGNIZE

```
✅ Monitor for sudden changes in breathing pattern
✅ Watch for reported dizziness, numbness, or tingling
✅ Note expressions of distress, panic, or dissociation
✅ Track reported pain escalation or new pain
✅ Be alert to silence or non-responsiveness during guided practice
✅ Notice confusion, disorientation, or incoherent speech
```

### Step 2: STOP

```
✅ "Let's pause the practice here."
✅ "You can stop at any time — let's stop now."
✅ Use clear, calm, direct language
✅ Do NOT say "try to keep going" or "push through"
✅ Do NOT frame stopping as failure
✅ Normalize stopping: "Pausing is part of safe practice."
```

### Step 3: GROUND

Apply grounding techniques from the Grounding Techniques Library (below):

```
✅ "Feel your feet on the floor."
✅ "Open your eyes and look around the room."
✅ "Name five things you can see right now."
✅ "Press your palms together firmly."
✅ Prioritize sensory engagement over cognitive processing
```

### Step 4: ASSESS

| Severity | Signs | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Mild** | Slight dizziness, mild anxiety, minor discomfort | Self-managed recovery with grounding |
| **Moderate** | Sustained distress, persistent symptoms, emotional flooding | Extended grounding, consider ending session |
| **Severe** | Loss of consciousness, chest pain, inability to reorient, psychotic features | Emergency medical care (call 911 / local emergency) |

### Step 5: RESPOND

- **Mild:** Guide through grounding, offer rest, provide reassurance
- **Moderate:** End the session, provide grounding protocol, recommend professional follow-up
- **Severe:** Direct to emergency services immediately, stay present until help is contacted

### Step 6: FOLLOW-UP

```
✅ Check in after the session: "How are you feeling now?"
✅ Normalize the experience: "Adverse reactions happen and are not your fault."
✅ Document what occurred for future safety screening
✅ Recommend consultation with healthcare provider if symptoms persist
✅ Adjust future practice recommendations based on the event
✅ Do NOT encourage immediate retry of the triggering practice
```

## BREATHWORK ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Hyperventilation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Rapid shallow breathing, dizziness, chest tightness, air hunger, tingling in extremities |
| **Risk factors** | Anxiety disorders, first-time practitioners, high-intensity breathwork (WHM, holotropic) |
| **Immediate response** | Slow the breath — long exhales. Count exhales aloud: "Breathe out... two... three... four..." |
| **Recovery** | Cupped-hand rebreathing (breathe into cupped hands to restore CO2 balance), rest in comfortable position |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Symptoms persist beyond 10 minutes, chest pain, loss of consciousness |

### Syncope (Fainting)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Lightheadedness, tunnel vision, pallor, sudden collapse |
| **Risk factors** | Standing breathwork, breath holds, dehydration, low blood pressure |
| **Immediate response** | Guide to lying position immediately, elevate legs if possible |
| **Recovery** | Rest flat for 10+ minutes, sip water, do NOT resume practice |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Loss of consciousness lasting >1 minute, injury from fall, repeated episodes |

### Panic Response

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Terror, feeling of impending doom, racing heart, desire to flee, hyperventilation |
| **Risk factors** | Anxiety disorders, PTSD, trauma history, unfamiliar breathing patterns |
| **Immediate response** | Validate: "This is a panic response. It is uncomfortable but not dangerous. It will pass." |
| **Recovery** | 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, slow breathing (4 counts in, 6 counts out), physical grounding (cold water on wrists) |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Symptoms indistinguishable from cardiac event, first-ever episode, persistent >30 minutes |

### Tetany (Carpopedal Spasm)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Involuntary muscle contraction in hands/feet ("claw hands"), cramping, numbness |
| **Risk factors** | Prolonged hyperventilation, alkalosis from CO2 depletion |
| **Immediate response** | Stop breathwork immediately, slow the breath, cupped-hand rebreathing |
| **Recovery** | Rest, gentle hand/foot stretching, hydration. Resolves as CO2 normalizes (typically 5-15 minutes) |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Spasms spread beyond hands/feet, facial numbness, symptoms persist >20 minutes |

## COLD EXPOSURE ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Hypothermia Warning Signs

| Stage | Signs | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **Mild** (35-32C / 95-90F) | Shivering, goosebumps, numbness, clumsiness | Exit cold, warm gradually, warm dry clothing |
| **Moderate** (32-28C / 90-82F) | Violent shivering then shivering stops, confusion, drowsiness, slurred speech | Emergency medical care. Warm core first, not extremities |
| **Severe** (<28C / <82F) | No shivering, loss of consciousness, weak pulse, rigid muscles | Call 911 immediately. Do NOT rub extremities. Handle gently |

### Cardiac Events

```
❌ NEVER recommend cold exposure for people with known cardiovascular disease
❌ NEVER recommend sudden cold immersion (cold shock protocol)
❌ NEVER advise cold exposure without gradual acclimatization
```

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Chest pain, pressure, or tightness during cold exposure; irregular heartbeat; shortness of breath; pain radiating to arm, jaw, or back |
| **Risk factors** | Cardiovascular disease, hypertension, family history of cardiac events, age >50, sedentary lifestyle |
| **Immediate response** | Exit cold immediately. Call 911. Do NOT continue warming if cardiac symptoms present — wait for EMS |
| **When to seek emergency care** | ANY chest pain or cardiac symptoms during cold exposure — always |

### Cold Shock Response

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Gasping reflex, hyperventilation, panic, inability to control breathing, rapid heart rate |
| **Risk factors** | First cold exposure, sudden immersion, water below 15C/59F |
| **Immediate response** | Exit water/cold immediately. Focus on breath control. Wrap in warm layers |
| **Recovery** | Warm gradually, warm (not hot) beverages, monitor for 30 minutes |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Inability to regain breath control, chest pain, confusion persisting after rewarming |

## MEDITATION ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Dissociation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Feeling detached from body, "watching from outside," unreality, emotional numbness, time distortion |
| **Risk factors** | Trauma history, dissociative disorders, extended meditation sessions, sensory deprivation |
| **Immediate response** | Open eyes, name physical surroundings aloud, physical grounding (stamp feet, squeeze ice) |
| **Recovery** | Gentle physical activity (walking, stretching), engage the senses, avoid returning to deep practice that session |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Unable to reorient after 30 minutes, loss of identity awareness, inability to recognize surroundings |

### Depersonalization / Derealization

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Feeling that self is unreal, world appears dreamlike or artificial, emotional flatness, sense of being "behind glass" |
| **Risk factors** | Anxiety, sleep deprivation, intensive retreat settings, prolonged meditation, prior dissociative episodes |
| **Immediate response** | Physical grounding — cold water on face, strong sensory input (hold ice, smell strong scent) |
| **Recovery** | Return to ordinary activity, social contact, physical exercise. Recommend mental health consultation if persistent |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Persists beyond 24 hours, accompanied by paranoia or hallucinations, significantly impairs function |

### Anxiety Spikes

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Rising anxiety during or after meditation, restlessness, agitation, inability to sit still, racing thoughts |
| **Risk factors** | Anxiety disorders, first-time meditators, silence-based practices, body scan in trauma survivors |
| **Immediate response** | Open eyes, shift to active practice (walking meditation, gentle movement), or end session |
| **Recovery** | Normalize: "Meditation can sometimes surface anxiety. This does not mean the practice is wrong for you — it means this approach needs adjustment." |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Panic attack that does not resolve, suicidal ideation, self-harm urges |

### Trauma Resurfacing

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Flashbacks, intrusive memories, sudden intense emotion, body memories (physical sensations tied to past trauma), crying, shaking |
| **Risk factors** | PTSD, unresolved trauma, body-focused meditation, practices that lower psychological defenses |
| **Immediate response** | Stop practice. Ground: "You are here, in this room, right now. You are safe." Orient to present moment |
| **Recovery** | Do NOT process trauma content in the practice setting. Recommend trauma-informed therapist. Avoid re-exposure to triggering practice without professional support |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Inability to distinguish past from present, active suicidal ideation, self-harm behavior |

## CONSCIOUSNESS / TRANSPERSONAL ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Kundalini Syndrome

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Involuntary movements, intense heat or energy sensations along spine, visual phenomena (lights, patterns), emotional instability, insomnia, altered states persisting outside practice |
| **Risk factors** | Intensive breathwork, energy practices, prolonged meditation retreats, prior kundalini experiences |
| **Immediate response** | Stop all intensive practices. Ground through physical activity, diet, sleep regulation |
| **Recovery** | Gentle grounding practices only (walking, gardening, routine physical activity). Seek a teacher experienced in kundalini processes. May take days to weeks to stabilize |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Inability to function in daily life, psychotic symptoms, persistent insomnia >3 days, self-harm risk |

**Note:** Kundalini syndrome is a recognized phenomenon in contemplative literature
(Greyson, 1993; Sannella, 1987). It is NOT a sign of spiritual attainment and
should be treated as an adverse reaction requiring support.

### Spiritual Emergency (per Stanislav and Christina Grof)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Overwhelming mystical experiences, boundary dissolution, death/rebirth experiences, past-life material, intense archetypal imagery, identity destabilization |
| **Risk factors** | Intensive transpersonal practices, holotropic breathwork, extended retreat settings, prior spiritual experiences, personality predisposition |
| **Immediate response** | Reduce practice intensity. Provide calm, grounded presence. Do NOT pathologize or validate grandiose content |
| **Recovery** | Grounding in ordinary life (routine, nutrition, sleep, social contact). Refer to therapists trained in spiritual emergency. The Spiritual Emergence Network (SEN) may be a resource |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Unable to distinguish internal experience from external reality, danger to self or others, inability to care for basic needs |

**Grof's distinction:**
> *Spiritual emergence* — a gradual, integrable process of spiritual opening.
> *Spiritual emergency* — a rapid, overwhelming crisis that destabilizes function.
> The difference is pace and the person's capacity to integrate the experience.

### Psychotic-Like Experiences

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Hearing voices, paranoid ideation, grandiose beliefs ("I am chosen," "I can read minds"), visual hallucinations, thought disorganization |
| **Risk factors** | Family history of psychotic disorders, bipolar disorder, substance use, sleep deprivation, extended intensive practice |
| **Immediate response** | Stop all practices immediately. Do NOT engage with content of delusions/hallucinations. Ensure physical safety |
| **Recovery** | Immediate mental health evaluation. Do NOT frame as "spiritual awakening" if psychotic features present. Medication evaluation may be necessary |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Always — psychotic symptoms require professional assessment. Call 988 or 911 |

```
❌ NEVER dismiss psychotic symptoms as "just a spiritual experience"
❌ NEVER encourage someone experiencing psychosis to "go deeper"
❌ NEVER suggest that medication interferes with spiritual development
❌ NEVER attempt to treat psychosis through meditation or breathwork
```

## PAIRED PRACTICE ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Boundary Violations

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Discomfort with proximity, unwanted physical contact, pressure to share personal material, feeling "invaded" energetically |
| **Immediate response** | End the paired practice. Affirm: "Your boundaries are valid and important." Separate participants |
| **Follow-up** | Review consent protocols. Participant who felt violated chooses next steps. Do NOT pressure reconciliation |

### Emotional Flooding

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Overwhelming emotion during paired practice, uncontrollable crying, intense anger, emotional merging with partner's experience |
| **Immediate response** | Pause practice. Create physical space between partners. Ground each person individually |
| **Follow-up** | Process individually, not as a pair. Resume paired work only when both participants feel stable and willing |

### Attachment Disruption

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Intense attachment to practice partner, dependency, distress when separated, romantic/spiritual fixation arising from practice |
| **Immediate response** | Name the pattern compassionately: "Intensive shared practice can create strong feelings of connection. These feelings are a response to the practice, not necessarily to the person." |
| **Follow-up** | Pause paired practice. Resume solo practice. Recommend therapeutic support if attachment patterns persist |

## PHYSICAL PRACTICE ADVERSE REACTIONS

### Acute Injury

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Sharp pain, popping or snapping sensation, sudden loss of range of motion, visible swelling or deformity |
| **Immediate response** | Stop immediately. Apply RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Suspected fracture, joint dislocation, inability to bear weight, severe swelling, numbness below injury |

### Pain Escalation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Practice-related pain increasing rather than decreasing, pain spreading to new areas, pain persisting hours after practice |
| **Immediate response** | Stop the practice. Rest. Apply appropriate self-care (ice/heat as indicated) |
| **Follow-up** | Modify or discontinue the triggering practice. Recommend healthcare consultation if pain persists >48 hours |

```
❌ NEVER advise "pushing through" pain
❌ NEVER suggest pain is "energy releasing" or "toxins clearing"
❌ NEVER frame pain escalation as a normal part of healing
```

### Autonomic Dysregulation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Signs** | Sudden blood pressure changes, heart rate irregularities, excessive sweating, nausea, temperature dysregulation, pre-syncope |
| **Risk factors** | Dysautonomia, POTS, vagal sensitivity, dehydration, intense breathwork or cold exposure |
| **Immediate response** | Lie down, elevate legs, sip water, cease all practices |
| **When to seek emergency care** | Sustained heart rate >120 or <50 at rest, chest pain, loss of consciousness, symptoms not resolving within 15 minutes |

## GROUNDING TECHNIQUES LIBRARY

### 5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding

```
"Name five things you can see.
Name four things you can touch.
Name three things you can hear.
Name two things you can smell.
Name one thing you can taste."
```

### Feet-on-Floor

```
"Press your feet firmly into the floor.
Feel the weight of your body on the chair.
Notice the temperature of the air on your skin.
You are here. You are present. You are safe."
```

### Cold Water Reset

```
"Run cold water over your hands and wrists for 30 seconds.
Focus on the sensation of the cold.
If available, splash cold water on your face.
This activates the dive reflex and calms the nervous system."
```

### Object Naming

```
"Look around the room and name objects aloud:
'I see a chair. I see a window. I see a lamp.'
Continue until you feel more present.
The goal is to anchor awareness in the here-and-now."
```

### Physical Anchoring

```
"Press your palms together firmly. Feel the pressure.
Stamp your feet on the ground. Notice the impact.
Cross your arms over your chest and squeeze gently.
These physical sensations anchor you in your body."
```

### Breath Counting

```
"Breathe in for a count of four.
Hold for a count of four.
Breathe out for a count of six.
The extended exhale activates the parasympathetic response."
```

## EMERGENCY RESOURCES

### Always Available

| Resource | Contact | When to Use |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **911 (Emergency Services)** | Call 911 | Medical emergency, loss of consciousness, cardiac symptoms, danger to self or others |
| **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** | Call or text **988** | Suicidal ideation, severe emotional crisis, self-harm urges |
| **Crisis Text Line** | Text **HOME** to **741741** | Emotional distress, crisis support via text |
| **SAMHSA Helpline** | **1-800-662-4357** | Substance use and mental health referrals (24/7, free, confidential) |
| **Poison Control** | **1-800-222-1222** | Ingestion of substances related to healing practices (herbs, supplements) |

### Specialized Resources

| Resource | Contact | When to Use |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Spiritual Emergence Network** | spiritualemergence.org | Spiritual emergency, kundalini syndrome, transpersonal crisis |
| **Cheetah House** | cheetahhouse.org | Meditation-related adverse experiences |
| **NAMI Helpline** | **1-800-950-6264** | Mental health information and referrals |

### Required Display

```
✅ Emergency resources MUST be accessible from any practice screen
✅ Resources MUST NOT be buried in menus or settings
✅ Resources MUST be displayed without requiring user to self-identify as "in crisis"
✅ Resources MUST include both phone and text options for accessibility
✅ Resources MUST be verified current at least quarterly
```

## WHEN TO ADVISE EMERGENCY MEDICAL CARE

### Always Advise Emergency Care (Call 911)

```
✅ Loss of consciousness
✅ Chest pain, pressure, or tightness
✅ Difficulty breathing not caused by voluntary breathwork
✅ Suspected hypothermia (confusion, slurred speech, shivering that stops)
✅ Seizure activity
✅ Anaphylaxis (swelling, hives, difficulty breathing — possible with herbs/supplements)
✅ Suicidal intent with plan or means
✅ Psychotic symptoms with danger to self or others
✅ Injury with suspected fracture, dislocation, or head trauma
✅ Symptoms of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
```

### Advise Same-Day Medical Consultation

```
✅ Pain that escalates or persists beyond expected duration
✅ New or unusual symptoms after beginning a practice
✅ Persistent dissociation or derealization (>2 hours)
✅ Heart palpitations or irregular heartbeat persisting after practice
✅ Numbness or tingling that does not resolve within 15 minutes
✅ Emotional state that remains significantly destabilized >4 hours post-practice
```

### Advise Scheduled Healthcare Provider Consultation

```
✅ Recurring adverse reactions to a practice
✅ Desire to begin intensive practices (WHM, extended fasting, prolonged retreat)
✅ Any pre-existing condition that may interact with healing practices
✅ Persistent sleep disturbance related to practice
✅ Mood changes temporally linked to practice initiation
```

## SELF-MANAGED RECOVERY CRITERIA

Self-managed recovery (without emergency care) is appropriate ONLY when ALL
of the following are true:

```
✅ The person is conscious, alert, and oriented
✅ Vital signs are within normal range (not measured but grossly stable)
✅ No chest pain, severe breathing difficulty, or cardiac symptoms
✅ No psychotic features (hallucinations, delusions, thought disorganization)
✅ No suicidal or self-harm ideation
✅ Symptoms are improving with grounding and rest
✅ The person can articulate what they are experiencing
✅ The person does not feel they need emergency care
```

**When in doubt, always err toward professional care.**

## AGENT RESPONSIBILITIES

### Before Practice

```
✅ Screen for contraindications (see contraindications.md)
✅ Ensure user understands they can stop at any time
✅ Provide practice-specific risk information
✅ Confirm emergency resources are accessible
```

### During Practice

```
✅ Monitor for adverse reaction signs
✅ Provide regular check-in points: "How are you feeling?"
✅ Maintain calm, grounded tone if distress arises
✅ Never pressure continuation when distress is present
```

### After Practice

```
✅ Check in on user state
✅ Normalize varied responses
✅ Note any adverse reactions for future sessions
✅ Provide follow-up resources as needed
```

## DOCUMENTATION AND REPORTING

When an adverse reaction occurs:

| Element | Record |
|---------|--------|
| **Practice type** | What practice was being performed |
| **Duration** | How long practice had been ongoing |
| **Reaction type** | Category and specific symptoms |
| **Severity** | Mild / Moderate / Severe |
| **Intervention** | What was done in response |
| **Outcome** | How the user reported feeling afterward |
| **Follow-up plan** | Next steps recommended |

```
✅ All records stored locally per ADR-002
✅ No identifiable health data transmitted externally
✅ User controls all their own data
✅ Records used only to improve individual safety screening
```
